## 1. Patron deities by faction type

A faction's patron deity sets its moral color and rites. For tone 3, the patron is often *ambiguous* or the faith is *bent* from the deity's true tenets. Full list: [AoN Deities](https://2e.aonprd.com/Deities.aspx).

| Faction type | Sincere / civic patron | Ambiguous patron | Shadow patron (the rot) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Guild / merchants** | Abadar (contracts, wealth) | Abadar (bent: greed over law) | Norgorber–Greed (the guild's hidden hand) |
| **Cult** | Sarenrae, Desna (benevolent sects) | Pharasma, Gozreh, Calistria | Urgathoa, Zon-Kuthon, Lamashtu, a Demon Lord |
| **Noble house** | Iomedae, Abadar | Calistria (vengeance), Norgorber (secrets) | Asmodeus (the infernal bargain) |
| **Criminal syndicate** | Cayden Cailean (folk-hero crews) | Calistria, Norgorber | Norgorber–Murder/Poison |
| **Knightly order / inquisition** | Iomedae, Torag, Sarenrae | Gorum (war as virtue), Irori (austere) | Zon-Kuthon (the order that became cruel) |
| **Rebel cell / revolution** | Cayden Cailean (liberty), Milani | Calistria (grievance) | a Demon Lord of freedom-as-chaos |
| **Cabal / secret society** | Irori (knowledge), Nethys | Nethys (magic without ethics), Norgorber | a Great Old One / Outer God (distant) |
| **Mercenary company** | Gorum (honest war) | Gorum, Norgorber | a daemon/Abaddon patron (war-as-nihilism) |

**Deity blocks worth knowing** (use the [AoN Deities](https://2e.aonprd.com/Deities.aspx) page, filter by category): Core 20 deities; **Archdevils** (Hell's hierarchy — perfect patrons for a corrupt noble house or a contract-bound order); **Demon Lords** (Abyss — chaotic horror cults); **The Eldest** (First World — fey cabals); **Empyreal Lords** (genuinely good orders); **Great Old Ones** (distant cosmic cults — use as *unseen* backer, never a daily presence).

## 2. Creatures as leadership & muscle

A faction's *teeth*. Link [AoN Creatures](https://2e.aonprd.com/Creatures.aspx) / [AoN Monster Families](https://2e.aonprd.com/MonsterFamilies.aspx).

### The hidden true leader (the twist behind the throne)
- **Vampire** — the reclusive patron, the noble who hosts only at night.
- **Lich / Mummy Lord** — the cabal's founder who never died; the order's "saint."
- **Hag (coven)** — the three wise-women who really run the guild.
- **Devil (e.g., a contract devil / higher devil)** — the silent partner holding the founding bargain.
- **Doppelganger / Faceless Stalker** — the lieutenant who isn't who they claim.
- **Rakshasa / Aboleth** — the manipulator pulling strings from comfort or the deep.

### The muscle / membership
- **Cultists, Cult Leaders, Acolytes** — the faithful (NPC blocks).
- **Wererats / Werewolves** — a syndicate or cell that's secretly a were-clan.
- **Skeletons / Zombies / Ghouls** — an Urgathoan or necromantic faction's labor.
- **Devils / Demons (lesser)** — summoned enforcers for an infernal/abyssal cult.
- **Mercenaries, Bandits, Spies, Assassins** — mortal muscle (NPC blocks).
- **Animated constructs / Homunculi** — a cabal's tireless servants.

## 3. Planar backers (who's *really* behind them)

Every supernatural faction traces to a plane — and "who backs them" is often the faction's deepest secret. See [AoN Planes](https://2e.aonprd.com/Planes.aspx).

- **Hell** → a contract-bound house/order; lawful corruption, infernal paperwork.
- **The Abyss / Outer Rifts** → a chaos cult; a rift, a summoning gone wrong.
- **Abaddon** → a nihilist/apocalypse faction; the doom they *want*.
- **The First World** → a fey cabal; old pacts, changeling agents.
- **The Shadow Plane** → a faction "gone wrong"; gothic, shadow-touched.
- **The Boneyard / Ethereal** → a death-cult or a faction of the unquiet dead.

**Pattern:** when a faction has supernatural power, ask "from where?" The answer (a plane, a pact, a relic) is usually the secret in Section 3e of the dossier and a prime PC leverage point.

## 4. Cult / order markers (texture)

Cheap ways to make a faction *feel* like its tone. From [AoN Languages](https://2e.aonprd.com/Languages.aspx) and trait conventions:

- **Languages as tells:** Infernal (devil-pacts), Abyssal/Daemonic (demon/daemon cults), Aklo (Old Ones — very dark), Necril (undead factions), Thassilonian (ancient lost-empire cabals).
- **Aesthetic/trait markers:** a recurring symbol; a color; a scar or brand; a coin left on the dead; a phrase members exchange. One concrete tell beats a paragraph of description.
- **Versatile-heritage agents** (for dark texture): Changeling (hag cabal), Dhampir (vampire's house), Nephilim–pitborn (infernal order), Duskwalker (death-cult). One such agent in the inner circle wires the faction's true nature into a face.

## 5. NPC stat-block anchors (don't invent numbers)

Sketch the leader and lieutenants' *personalities*; for mechanics, point at a reusable PF2e NPC block (Monster Core / NPC Gallery), found on [AoN Creatures](https://2e.aonprd.com/Creatures.aspx):

| Faction role | Reusable PF2e NPC block to suggest |
|---|---|
| Guild master / merchant prince | Noble, Merchant |
| Cult leader / prophet | Cult Leader, Acolyte, Adept |
| Crime boss | Crime Lord, Bandit Lord |
| Knight-commander / inquisitor | Knight, Captain, Inquisitor-style block |
| Spymaster / cabal agent | Spy, Charlatan, Assassin |
| Rebel leader | Bandit Lord, Rabble-rouser/Commoner reskinned |
| Mercenary captain | Mercenary, Knight, Captain |
| Rank-and-file | Guard, Cultist, Bandit, Thug, Commoner |

Tell the user: "Use the **[role]** block as-is or bump its level to fit the party." Don't write attack bonuses or HP yourself.

---

## Anti-pattern reminder
A faction is **drive + motion**, not a roster. Reach into this file to anchor *who backs them* and *what their teeth are* — not to inventory every member. The PF2e canon should sharpen the faction's identity and secret, not pad it.
