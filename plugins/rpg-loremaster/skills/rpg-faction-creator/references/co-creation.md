# Co-Creation & Read-Aloud (faction-creator)

> Operational copy of the kit's shared DNA (master: `design-philosophy.md` at the kit root). This is the **front-end** of the skill — run it before generating. The skill is a creative partner that draws the GM's ideas out, sharpens them, and fills gaps; it is **not** a vending machine. Interviewing the user across several rounds is good, not a failure — as long as it's *elicitation*, not interrogation.

## Part A — The co-creation loop

### Read the room first
Gauge how much the user brought and how much they want to do themselves, then set the mode:
- **Rich seed + clearly wants it built** → light-touch: reflect it back, fill gaps, confirm, generate.
- **Sparse seed, or "help me think this through"** → deeper elicitation; draw the vision out before generating.
- **"Just generate it / surprise me"** → autonomous mode: pick sensible defaults, name them aloud, generate, offer to revisit.

State the **escape hatch early**: "At any point say 'just run with it' and I'll fill in the rest." The user must never feel trapped in the interview.

### The loop — repeat per load-bearing decision
1. **Open.** Ask a broad question that pulls *their* image first. "What does this faction *want* — and what do they tell themselves they're doing?" before "how big is it?"
2. **Spark when stuck.** If they have no answer, offer 2–4 concrete, evocative options to pick or remix (use AskUserQuestion). Options are launchpads to react against — the *lapidar* step — not a menu to settle for.
3. **Reflect.** Play their answer back, sharper. "So they're less 'a thieves' guild' and more 'a mutual-aid society for the poor that funds itself by robbing the rich and has started to enjoy the robbing more than the aid' — yes?"
4. **Probe gaps & tensions.** Surface what's missing or contradictory and offer options. "You want them noble *and* feared — is the fear a misunderstanding, or have they done something that earned it?"
5. **Generate by section, check in.** When co-creating, build one section, show it, invite course-correction before moving to the next. Don't dump the whole dossier mid-collaboration.

### The load-bearing decisions for a faction (ask about these; default the rest)
In priority order — the drive is the engine everything grows from, so anchor the vision there first:
1. **The drive** — the goal and, more importantly, the *impulse* (the why beneath) and the *ideology* (who benefits if they win). Spend the most elicitation here.
2. **The plan in motion** — what they're doing right now, and the doom if the PCs never intervene.
3. **The leader and the fracture** — who runs it (and what *they* personally want), and where the faction is split.
4. **Type, reach, and tone** — guild/cult/house/syndicate/order/cell; how far their power extends; tone defaults to 3.

**Default freely:** exact lieutenant names, the precise AoN creature for muscle, the symbol's exact look, secondary resources — unless the user signals they care.

### The test for "am I interrogating?"
Interrogation is blank questions in a row. Elicitation is open questions + sparks + reflection, one thread at a time, always moving the creation forward. **If you've asked two questions without giving something back** (a reflection, an option, a generated beat), you've slipped into interrogation. *Give before you ask again.*

### Worked micro-example (the drive)
> **You:** "Before structure or names — what does this cult actually *want*, and what do its members believe they're doing?"
> **User:** "They worship a drowned god. They think the flood is coming and they want to be ready."
> **You (spark):** "Good. 'Ready' is the lever — three flavors, pick or bend: (a) *they want to save everyone and are building an ark nobody asked for*, (b) *they've decided who deserves to be saved and are quietly culling the list*, (c) *they want the flood to come faster and are sabotaging the seawalls to prove their faith.*"
> **User:** "B, but they think they're being merciful."
> **You (reflect + gap):** "So the impulse is mercy and the practice is murder — they're choosing who drowns and calling it grace. That's a perfect level-3 rot: arguable from the inside, monstrous from the outside. One thing before I draft: is the leader a true believer or a cynic riding the faith? That changes the whole reaction ladder."

## Part B — Read-aloud narration (beats-key density)

A faction is less location-bound than a city, so its read-aloud is about **presence and voice** — how the PCs *feel* the faction in the world. Mark each block as a blockquote labelled **Read-aloud** (PT-BR: **Para ler à mesa**). Match the user's language.

### The rules (from published-adventure best practice)
- **Short** — ~40–70 words.
- **Sensory** — what the senses catch right now: the mark on the wall, the enforcer's voice, the smell after the rite.
- **Never narrate the PCs** — don't state what the characters feel, think, do, or will do. (Exception: an explicit magical compulsion.)
- **Don't predict their future** — no "you'll soon cross them".
- **End open** — trail off on something interactive or unresolved.
- **Test aloud** — phrasing must read smoothly spoken.

### Where read-aloud goes in a faction dossier (the beats)
- **The calling card** — how the PCs first encounter evidence of the faction (the establishing beat): graffiti over a rival's door, an enforcer collecting a debt, the aftermath of a rite, a recruiter's pitch.
- **The leader's voice** — one quoted line in their voice (doubles as the voice hook).
- **One signature moment** — a ritual, a faction meeting, a reprisal — a scene the GM can drop in when the faction acts.

Keep them offerings, not scripts — the GM will riff. Don't read-aloud every rank or resource; convey presence and voice, not a tour.

### Example (a calling card, ~55 words)
> **Read-aloud / Para ler à mesa:**
> *The moneylender's door is painted shut — not boarded, painted, a thick tidemark of blue-green sealing the frame like the sea closed it. A wet handprint trails up the wood to a single word pressed into the paint: SAVED. Down the street, three more doors wear the same mark. None of the neighbors will look at them.*

### Example (a leader voice line)
> **Mother Cholva** (voice): > *"I am not cruel, child. The water is cruel. I am only deciding who it takes — and that is the kindest thing anyone has ever done for this city."*
