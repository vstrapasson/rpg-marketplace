# Tone Spectrum — the 1–5 Dark-Leaning Scale (faction edition)

Defines the five tone levels this skill can generate. Default is **level 3 (dark-leaning)**. Read this whenever generating at a level other than 3, or whenever unsure how much shadow to put into a faction. (Mirrors the kit's shared scale, reframed for faction elements — drive, rot, the leader, the doom.)

## The scale

| Level | Label | Reference works | What it feels like |
|---|---|---|---|
| **1** | Luminous classical | Forgotten Realms guilds, Dragonlance orders | Clean motives, clear villains, the good faction is simply good. |
| **2** | Heroic adventure | Pathfinder default factions, Eberron's dragonmarked houses | Real interests, mild moral complications, but you can tell who to trust. |
| **3 ⭐ default** | **Dark-leaning** | Curse of Strahd's factions, Wrath of the Righteous cults, Witcher 3 (Novigrad's powers) | Sincere ideals with a rotted edge; a faction you can argue *for* and still fear. Light and shadow alternate. |
| **4** | Dark fantasy | Berserk's Holy See, darker Witcher factions | Most factions are compromised; the "good" option is the lesser evil. |
| **5** | Grimdark | Warhammer's Inquisition, ASOIAF's great houses at worst | Every faction is a predator; joining any of them costs your soul. |

## How each level changes the faction

### Level 1 (Luminous classical)
- The drive is clean; the ideology needs no defending.
- No internal rot; the faction is what it claims to be.
- The leader is straightforwardly admirable (or straightforwardly a villain, if it's an enemy faction).
- The impending doom is an external threat the faction *fights*, not one it *causes*.
- Hooks are "help the good guild / stop the obvious cult."

### Level 2 (Heroic adventure)
- One moral complication (a rival the faction treats unfairly; a compromise they made).
- A minor internal disagreement, not a schism.
- The secret is mild (a financial irregularity, a hidden romance).
- 1–2 hooks carry ambiguity.

### Level 3 ⭐ (Dark-leaning — DEFAULT)
- **Mix 5:3:2** — the faction is mostly sincere, with ambiguity and **one** clearly rotted part.
- **The sympathizer test is mandatory:** you can state the case *for* the faction even if it's an antagonist.
- **Exactly one rotted part** (a compromised leader, a hardline wing, a monstrous cost of membership) — the rest sincere, so the rot is a *discovery*.
- **The impending doom is something the faction itself causes** (not just an external threat) — and the faction believes it's the right thing.
- The leader is **arguable from inside, troubling from outside** (mercy that kills; order that crushes).
- At least one secret gives the PCs real leverage.
- At least 1–2 hooks carry a moral cost — opposing the faction helps someone worse, or the faction is right about the wrong thing.
- A paladin of Iomedae should be able to *almost* join before the truth surfaces.

### Level 4 (Dark fantasy)
- Mix flips to 2:3:5 — the faction is mostly shadow with a few sincere holdouts.
- The rot is no longer one part; it's structural. The clean members are the exception and are in danger.
- The leader is compromised or cynical; the founding ideal is mostly a memory.
- The doom is dire and the obvious ways to stop it are all ugly.
- Hooks routinely force the lesser evil.

### Level 5 (Grimdark)
- Mix 0:2:8 — no clean part remains.
- The drive is predatory; the ideology is a justification, not a belief.
- Every lieutenant is a threat; membership is a trap.
- The doom is near-unstoppable; "winning" means choosing who it lands on.
- Add a content advisory in the off-stage notes.

## Applying level 3 specifically (the default checklist)

1. **Sympathizer test:** can you argue *for* this faction in one sentence? If not, add a true grievance or a real good they protect.
2. **One rotted part, rest sincere.** Name it; keep the rest clean for contrast.
3. **The faction causes its own doom** and thinks it's right.
4. **The leader is arguable-from-inside.** Mercy/duty/faith that curdles, not cartoon evil.
5. **A usable secret** gives the PCs leverage.
6. **≥1–2 moral-cost hooks** — mark them.
7. **The cost of membership** carries quiet darkness.

The goal of level 3 is **contrast**: a faction sincere enough to join and rotted enough to fear. Pure light = a quest-giver. Pure shadow = a monster to kill. Dark-leaning = a choice that costs.

## Common mistakes

- **Over-darkening.** If the user asked for 3 and you wrote a faction no decent person could join, you wrote a 4–5. Pull back: restore the sincere majority.
- **Under-darkening.** If the faction has no rot, no usable secret, and no moral-cost hook, it's a 1–2. Add the rotted part and make one hook hurt.
- **Decorative darkness.** Don't bolt on blood-rites for shock. Every shadow element ties to the drive, the rot, or a hook. The rot should *follow from* the impulse (a fear-driven faith turns to culling; a grievance-driven cell turns to terror).

## Sliding between levels
If the user wants a level-3 faction but "darker on the inner circle," generate at base 3 and dial that section up by one. Note the deviation in off-stage so downstream skills stay consistent.
