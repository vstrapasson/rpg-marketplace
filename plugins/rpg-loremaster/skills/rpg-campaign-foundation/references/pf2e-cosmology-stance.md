# PF2e Cosmological Stance — adopt and spin, don't invent

How to ground a campaign's **central truth** in Pathfinder 2e's existing cosmology instead of building metaphysics from scratch. This is the answer to "should I build a world when I use ready-made materials?" — **no.** You *adopt* PF2e's Great Beyond, pantheon, and bestiary and decide your campaign's **stance and spin** on them. Original metaphysics is a rare, explicit override.

> **About the examples here:** illustrative only — generic, or from published media (e.g. Critical Role's *Vox Machina*). **Never reuse them verbatim;** pick the canon *your* campaign's truth needs and invent your own spin.

## 1. The principle — stance, not construction

When you use a published setting's canon (PF2e/Golarion, or D&D), the metaphysics is **already done and richer than you'd build under deadline**. Your creative work is not to invent a cosmology — it's to:
1. **Pick** the one or two canon elements your campaign's truth *hinges on*.
2. **Spin** them — decide your campaign's specific, often unsettling take.
3. **Reference** them (AoN) so you and your players share the canon.

A published example of the move: Critical Role's *Vox Machina* invents *no* new cosmology — it takes an existing god of secrets and undeath (Vecna) and builds its late campaign around that power's bid to ascend. In a PF2e game you'd do exactly the same with an existing deity (say **Norgorber** for secrets, or **Urgathoa** for undeath): adopt the canon, spin one truth onto it, and let the campaign hang off that single spin.

**You build cosmology only if** the user explicitly wants an original world. Even then, lean on PF2e's *structures* (an analog of the Boneyard, of a judge-of-the-dead) rather than reinventing from zero.

## 2. Deities as campaign engines

The fastest way to a campaign truth is to ask: **what if a thing the faithful believe about this god is wrong — or hidden?** PF2e's pantheon is full of engines. (Full list: [AoN Deities](https://2e.aonprd.com/Deities.aspx).) *(Spins below are illustrative — invent your own.)*

| Deity | Domain | Campaign-engine spin (examples — pick or invent your own) |
|---|---|---|
| **Pharasma** | death, birth, prophecy, the soul cycle | a rash of botched resurrections is fraying fate; a psychopomp has abandoned its post and the dead won't stay down; her prophecy names a city's last day |
| **Asmodeus** | tyranny, contracts, Hell | an ancient contract is coming due on a whole city; a "lawful" order is enforcing an infernal bargain |
| **Norgorber** | secrets, murder, the Four faces | a secret the god is *keeping* is the campaign's truth; a cabal is killing to bury knowledge; a hidden bid to ascend |
| **Urgathoa** | undeath, disease, gluttony | a plague is being sold as a sacrament; undeath offered as "mercy" in a doomed town |
| **Zon-Kuthon** | pain, darkness, loss | a region's grief was bargained into something worse; shadow leaking into the world |
| **Lamashtu** | monstrous birth, nightmares | what's being born; the "monsters" are her blessing, the village the aberration |
| **Gozreh / Gorum / Calistria** | nature / war / vengeance | indifferent nature turning hostile; a war-cult's glory hiding rot; a centuries-old vengeance maturing |
| **Iomedae / Sarenrae / Torag** (heroic) | virtue / redemption / protection | the *institution* of the faith has rotted while the god stays silent; a holy war built on a lie |

**Heroic deities make great engines too** — a dark-leaning campaign often turns on a *good* faith whose **institution** failed (the church that buried a crime, the order that overreached). The god isn't the villain; the cover-up is.

## 3. Planes as campaign engines

Where a supernatural truth *comes from*. (Full list: [AoN Planes](https://2e.aonprd.com/Planes.aspx).)
- **The Boneyard** (Pharasma's realm of judgment) — the unquiet dead, a judgment withheld, a soul that must be retrieved or that escaped.
- **The Shadow Plane** — a region/truth "gone wrong"; a creeping wrongness; gothic dread.
- **Hell / The Abyss / Abaddon** — a contract, a rift, a nihilist doom (Abaddon = the end the villain *wants*).
- **The First World** — old pacts, fae bargains, a truth older than the current age.
- **The Ethereal / Negative-Void** — hauntings, why the dead persist, a place that drains the living.

**Pattern:** a campaign's supernatural truth almost always traces to *one* plane. Naming it (Hell, for a contract campaign; the First World, for an old-bargain campaign) gives the truth a source, a flavor, and a destination the PCs might eventually reach.

## 4. Creature families as the threat's nature

What the antagonist *is* shapes the whole tone. (See [AoN Creatures](https://2e.aonprd.com/Creatures.aspx) / [AoN Monster Families](https://2e.aonprd.com/MonsterFamilies.aspx).)
- **Undead (lich, graveknight, mummy lord)** — the past refusing to stay buried; an ancient agenda outliving its era.
- **Fiends (devils/demons/daemons)** — a bargain, a corruption, an apocalypse depending on the lower plane.
- **Aberrations** — a cosmic-horror truth; something that shouldn't be, knowing something it shouldn't.
- **Dragons / Hags / Fey** — an ancient agenda playing out over centuries; a bargain or a grudge with deep roots. (*Vox Machina*'s Chroma Conclave is the dragon version writ large.)

Pick the family whose *nature* embodies the truth.

## 5. How to write the stance

In the dossier's "PF2e Cosmological Stance" section, keep it to four lines:
- **Hinges on:** [deity/deities] — [one line on the canon you're using]
- **Plane(s):** [the plane the truth comes from / leads to]
- **Threat's nature:** [creature family] — [why it fits the truth]
- **The spin:** [your campaign's specific, often unsettling take on that canon — the one sentence that makes it *yours*]

Then link the AoN category pages so the table shares the canon. That's the whole stance — adopted, spun, referenced. No cosmology built.
