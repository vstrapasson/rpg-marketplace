# Tone Spectrum — the 1–5 Dark-Leaning Scale (NPC edition)

Defines the five tone levels this skill can generate. Default is **level 3 (dark-leaning)**. Read this whenever generating at a level other than 3, or whenever unsure how much shadow to put into a character. (Mirrors the kit's shared scale, reframed for NPC elements — the contradiction, surface vs. beneath, the motive, and how much sympathy the character earns.) **System-agnostic** — this is a tonal dial, not a mechanical one.

## The scale

| Level | Label | Reference characters | What it feels like |
|---|---|---|---|
| **1** | Luminous classical | the wise mentor, the noble knight, the kindly innkeeper | Clean, coherent people; the good ones are simply good, villains simply bad. |
| **2** | Heroic adventure | most Pathfinder NPCs, a flawed-but-decent sheriff | Real personalities, a mild flaw or two, but you can tell who to trust. |
| **3 ⭐ default** | **Dark-leaning** | Curse of Strahd's villagers, Witcher 3's Bloody Baron, a mentor with a hidden agenda | Sincere on the surface with a rotted edge beneath; a person you can sympathize with and still distrust. Surface and depth pull against each other. |
| **4** | Dark fantasy | Berserk's Griffith, a "good" lord who does terrible necessary things | Mostly compromised; their kindness is real but the cost they exact is high. |
| **5** | Grimdark | ASOIAF at its cruelest, a charming monster | A predator wearing a person; sympathy is a trap the character sets. |

## How each level changes the NPC

### Level 1 (Luminous classical)
- Surface and depth match — what you see is what they are.
- No real contradiction; the motive needs no defending.
- The wound (if any) is healed and ennobling, not active.
- A villain at this level is straightforwardly, knowably evil.

### Level 2 (Heroic adventure)
- One genuine flaw that occasionally complicates things (a temper, a vice, a blind spot).
- A small gap between surface and depth, easily read.
- The secret is mild (an embarrassment, a hidden softness).

### Level 3 ⭐ (Dark-leaning — DEFAULT)
- **Mix 5:3:2** — the person is mostly sincere, with real ambiguity and **one** genuinely troubling part.
- **The sympathizer test is mandatory:** you can state the case *for* this person — including a villain — in one sentence.
- **Surface vs. beneath:** a clear gap between how they present and who they are, and the gap *does work* (it's the thing players want to understand).
- **The motive is specific and the wound is active** — the past still shapes today's behavior, not just their résumé.
- **They cause their own complications** — the flaw makes their life (and the PCs') harder; it isn't a cute quirk.
- At least one **secret or blind spot** gives the PCs leverage or leverage *over* them.
- At least 1–2 hooks carry a **moral cost** — helping them harms someone, or opposing them harms someone sympathetic.
- A decent person at the table should be able to *almost* trust them before the truth surfaces.

### Level 4 (Dark fantasy)
- Mix flips to 2:3:5 — mostly shadow, with a few sincere embers.
- The contradiction tips toward darkness: their virtue is real but subordinate to something colder.
- The wound runs the show; they hurt people the way they were hurt.
- Sympathy survives, but trusting them is clearly a mistake.

### Level 5 (Grimdark)
- Mix 0:2:8 — no clean part remains.
- Charm and warmth are instruments; the motive is predatory.
- The "contradiction" is the mask itself.
- Add a content advisory in the off-stage notes.

## Applying level 3 specifically (the default checklist)

1. **Sympathizer test:** can you argue *for* this person in one sentence? If not, give them a true grievance, a real love, or a good they protect.
2. **One genuine contradiction**, surface vs. beneath — name it; keep the rest coherent for contrast.
3. **Want vs. need:** a conscious goal *and* a deeper need they won't admit — the tension between them is the drama.
4. **The wound is active** and connects to current behavior (cause → effect), not just a sad résumé.
5. **A usable secret or blind spot** gives the PCs leverage.
6. **≥1–2 moral-cost hooks** — mark them.
7. **The flaw makes problems** — for them and, ideally, for the PCs.

The goal of level 3 is **contrast**: a person sincere enough to root for and rotted enough to distrust. Pure light = a quest-dispenser. Pure shadow = a monster to kill. Dark-leaning = a person whose help (or harm) costs something.

## Common mistakes

- **Over-darkening.** If the user asked for 3 and you wrote someone no decent person could stand, you wrote a 4–5. Pull back: restore the sincere majority and the thing worth rooting for.
- **Under-darkening.** If the NPC has no contradiction, no usable secret, and no moral-cost hook, it's a 1–2. Add the gap between surface and depth, and make one hook hurt.
- **Decorative darkness (the "sad backstory = depth" trap).** Don't bolt on tragedy for flavor. Every shadow element must *connect to current behavior* — the wound produced a coping mechanism, a blind spot, a defense. A tragic past that changes nothing about how they act now is set dressing.

## Sliding between levels
If the user wants a level-3 NPC but "darker once you really know them," generate at base 3 and dial the *beneath* up by one while keeping the surface at 3. Note the deviation in off-stage so downstream skills and the reviewer stay consistent.
