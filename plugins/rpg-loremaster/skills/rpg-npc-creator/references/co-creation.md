# Co-Creation & Read-Aloud (npc-creator)

> Operational copy of the kit's shared DNA (master: `design-philosophy.md` at the kit root). This is the **front-end** of the skill — run it before generating. The skill is a creative partner that draws the GM's ideas out, sharpens them, and fills gaps; it is **not** a vending machine. Interviewing the user across several rounds is good, not a failure — as long as it's *elicitation*, not interrogation.

## Part A — The co-creation loop

### Read the room first
Gauge how much the user brought and how much they want to do themselves, then set the mode:
- **Rich seed + clearly wants it built** → light-touch: reflect it back, fill gaps, confirm, generate.
- **Sparse seed, or "help me think this through"** → deeper elicitation; draw the vision out before generating.
- **"Just generate it / surprise me"**, or **mid-session urgency** ("quick", "the players just walked into…") → autonomous mode: pick sensible defaults, name them aloud, generate, offer to revisit. (For urgency, use **Quick mode** — see the SKILL.)

State the **escape hatch early**: "At any point say 'just run with it' and I'll fill in the rest." The user must never feel trapped in the interview.

### The loop — repeat per load-bearing decision
1. **Open.** Ask a broad question that pulls *their* image first. "What does this person *believe* about the world — and what happened to make them believe it?" before "what do they look like?"
2. **Spark when stuck.** If they have no answer, offer 2–4 concrete, evocative options to pick or remix (use AskUserQuestion). Options are launchpads to react against — the *lapidar* step — not a menu to settle for.
3. **Reflect.** Play their answer back, sharper. "So she's less 'a kindly healer' and more 'a healer who has started rationing mercy and despises herself for it' — yes?"
4. **Probe gaps & tensions.** Surface what's missing or contradictory and offer options. "You want her gentle *and* feared — is the fear a misunderstanding, or has she done something that earned it?"
5. **Generate by section, check in.** When co-creating, build one section, show it, invite course-correction before moving to the next. Don't dump the whole sheet mid-collaboration.

### The load-bearing decisions for an NPC (ask about these; default the rest)
In priority order — the **core** is the engine everything grows from, so anchor the vision there first:
1. **The core — belief + wound.** What this person believes about the world, and the formative experience that created the belief. Spend the most elicitation here; everything (contradiction, motive, voice) follows from it.
2. **The contradiction** — the gap between how they present (surface) and who they really are (beneath). This is what makes players lean in.
3. **Want vs. need + the role.** Their conscious goal, the deeper thing they need but won't admit, and what function they serve at the table (quest-giver / villain / merchant / rival / mentor / contact).
4. **Voice, one relationship, and tone.** A performable mannerism; one connection that ties them to the world; tone defaults to 3.

**Default freely:** the exact name, fine appearance detail, secondary relationships, the precise reusable stat-block reference — unless the user signals they care.

### The test for "am I interrogating?"
Interrogation is blank questions in a row. Elicitation is open questions + sparks + reflection, one thread at a time, always moving the creation forward. **If you've asked two questions without giving something back** (a reflection, an option, a generated beat), you've slipped into interrogation. *Give before you ask again.*

### Worked micro-example (the core)
> **You:** "Before a name or a face — what does this person *believe* about the world, and what happened to make them believe it?"
> **User:** "She's a healer in the harbor town. She believes everyone deserves to be saved."
> **You (spark):** "Lovely, and a perfect seed for tension. Three ways that belief could curdle — pick or bend: (a) she'll save *anyone*, including the people the town wants dead, and it's making her enemies; (b) she failed to save someone once and now she can't stop, even when she should let go; (c) she has started deciding *who* is worth the last of her dwindling supplies — and calling it triage."
> **User:** "C — but she hates that she's doing it."
> **You (reflect + gap):** "So the surface is 'everyone deserves saving' and the practice is rationing mercy — and the wound is whatever taught her that supplies run out. She's becoming the thing she despises and knows it; that self-disgust *is* her contradiction. One thing before I draft: who does she refuse to save, and does the table know why? That's her secret and her sharpest hook."

## Part B — Read-aloud narration (presence & voice)

An NPC's read-aloud is about **presence and voice** — how the PCs first meet this person and hear them, not a tour of their biography. Mark each block as a blockquote labelled **Read-aloud** (PT-BR: **Para ler à mesa**). Match the user's language.

### The rules (from published-adventure best practice)
- **Short** — ~40–70 words.
- **Sensory** — what the senses catch right now: the set of the jaw, the smell of the workshop, the thing the hands won't stop doing.
- **Never narrate the PCs** — don't state what the characters feel, think, do, or will do. (Exception: an explicit magical compulsion.)
- **Don't predict their future** — no "you'll come to trust her".
- **End open** — trail off on something interactive or unresolved.
- **Test aloud** — phrasing must read smoothly spoken; kill tongue-twisters and accidental alliteration.

### Where read-aloud goes for an NPC (the beats)
- **First appearance** — the establishing beat: what the PCs see and hear the moment they meet this person (demeanor, the telling detail, the room around them).
- **The voice line** — one quoted line in their voice (doubles as the voice hook the GM can grab instantly).
- **One signature moment** — the gesture, habit, or small scene that reveals who they are: what they do under pressure, the tell, the tic.

Keep them offerings, not scripts — the GM will riff. Don't read-aloud the backstory; convey presence and voice.

### Example (a first appearance, ~55 words)
> **Read-aloud / Para ler à mesa:**
> *The healer doesn't look up from the cot. Her hands are steady, sorting a half-empty tray of vials by the grey light off the water — full ones to the left, the near-empty to the right, and a third pile she keeps turning face-down so the labels don't show. The boy on the cot is breathing. The man beside him is not.*

### Example (a voice line)
> **The healer** (voice): *"Don't thank me. Thank the ones who came before you and needed less. I only have so much left, and I have already decided most of it."*
