# Coherence Rules

The bedrock principle: **every element in the region must be plausible given the geography, OR be explicitly justified.** Silent contradictions break immersion; justified contradictions become features.

## The causal chain

Build outward from geography. Each step constrains what's plausible in the next.

```
Geography (biome + climate)
    ↓
Resources & barriers
    ↓
Ancestries (who plausibly settled)
    ↓
Religion (who plausibly thrives)
    ↓
Fauna (what plausibly evolved/migrated)
    ↓
Factions (what plausibly organizes)
    ↓
Politics & history (what plausibly happened)
    ↓
Hooks (what plausibly matters)
```

Skipping steps creates incoherence. Working in this order, by default, gives you grounded results.

## Biome → ancestries (suggested fits)

Use as a *starting point*, not a constraint. Any ancestry can live anywhere with the right justification (humans always work).

| Biome | Likely dominant | Plausible minority | Unlikely (needs justification) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Temperate forest | Human, Elf, Halfling | Gnome, Leshy, Dwarf (mining) | Goblin (in numbers — bands ok), Orc (unless frontier) |
| Cold mountain | Human (hill folk), Dwarf, Orc | Goblin (Snow), Half-Orc/Dromaar | Elf (unless ancient), Leshy (unless Cactus/Fungus) |
| Cold tundra/arctic | Human (Wintertouched), Dwarf (Anvil) | Goblin (Snow), Orc (Winter) | Most others without explanation |
| Hot desert | Human (desert kin), Gnoll | Goblin (Charhide), Catfolk, Dwarf (canyon) | Elf, Halfling, Leshy (unless Cactus) |
| Swamp/marsh | Lizardfolk, Boggard, Human | Halfling, Leshy (Vine, Seapod) | Most others — swamps are hostile |
| Coast/sea | Human, Halfling, Merfolk/Azarketi | Elf (sailor), Leshy (Seapod), Tengu | Dwarf (unless trade), Orc |
| Jungle | Human (Mwangi-styled), Anadi, Leshy | Gnome (First World), Catfolk | Dwarf, most cold-adapted |
| Underground | Dwarf (Cavernous), Drow, Duergar | Goblin, Kobold, Orc (Deep) | Elf (unless ancient/changed), Halfling |
| Urban (city-region) | Mixed everything | All Versatile Heritages | None — cities mix freely |
| Planar borderlands | Heritage-marked (Nephilim, Duskwalker, Changeling) | Human + planar versatile | Pure-blooded anything (the place changes you) |

**Justification patterns** for off-fit ancestries:
- *Diaspora*: "the elves are here because their old homeland was destroyed, and they took refuge with the local humans three generations ago"
- *Trade*: "the dwarves built a quarter in this coastal city because the city's a major export hub for their iron"
- *Refuge*: "the goblins fled here when [larger force] burned their forest — they live in the broken houses on the edge of town"
- *Exile*: "the orc warband is here because they were cast out by their own clan after a failed coup, and the local lord pays them to patrol the border"
- *Origin*: "the leshy of the Bog were grown by a druidic circle a century ago and have outlived the druids"

When you place an off-fit ancestry, give a one-line justification. It's the difference between random and intentional.

## Biome → likely deities

Deities feel right when they fit the landscape and the people's daily concerns.

| Biome / context | Strongly fitting | Often present | Unusual (needs justification) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Mountains | Torag, Gorum (warrior cultures) | Erastil (rural), Pharasma | Gozreh, Desna |
| Forests | Erastil, Gozreh, Calistria (elven), Eldest | Desna, Shelyn | Asmodeus (unless infiltrating), Torag |
| Deserts | Sarenrae, Abadar (caravan cities) | Gorum, Norgorber | Gozreh, Erastil (subsistence farming hard) |
| Coast / sea | Gozreh, Desna (travel), Cayden | Pharasma (sailors die a lot), Shelyn | Torag, Erastil |
| Cold north | Erastil (survival), Gorum, Torag | Pharasma, Desna | Sarenrae, Shelyn |
| Underground | Torag, Dwarven gods, Pharasma | Norgorber (secrets), Nethys | Most surface-deities |
| Urban centers | Abadar, Norgorber, Shelyn, Calistria | Iomedae, Sarenrae (cathedrals), Asmodeus (politics) | Erastil (more rural) |
| Frontier wilds | Erastil, Cayden, Desna | Gorum, Lamashtu (cults) | Highly organized faiths (Iomedae as institution rare) |
| Necropoleis / death-marked | Pharasma (default), Urgathoa (cult) | Zon-Kuthon, Norgorber | Sarenrae, Shelyn |
| Dark-leaning regions (general) | Pharasma, Calistria, Norgorber | Asmodeus, Urgathoa, Zon-Kuthon | Sarenrae as dominant (possible but textured) |

## Faction orthogonality

The single biggest failure mode for factions is binary structure — "good guys" vs "bad guys". Every region should pass this test:

**For any pair of factions A and B, their conflict should differ from any pair (A, C) or (B, C).**

If A clashes with B for the same reason A clashes with C, you have one faction (the protagonist) and two enemies. That's flat.

Examples of orthogonal sets (3 factions):

✅ **Good:**
- The Merchants' Council wants safe trade routes
- The Druidic Circle wants the woods left alone
- The Bishop's Office wants pilgrims paying tithes

→ Merchants vs. Druids: road expansion through sacred grove
→ Merchants vs. Bishop: who taxes the caravans
→ Druids vs. Bishop: the church's land hunger

Every pair fights about something different. Players can side with anyone and create new conflict.

❌ **Bad:**
- The Knights of Light (heroes)
- The Cult of Shadow (villains)
- The Bandits (also villains)

→ All conflicts collapse to "heroes vs. wrong people". Players have one direction.

When you generate factions, sanity-check by writing the conflicts pairwise. If two of them sound the same, redesign one.

## Politics: de jure vs. de facto

The gap between official authority and real power is one of the cheapest sources of dark-leaning texture. Patterns:

- **A puppet ruler**: Duke X holds the throne; his uncle / mother / first minister actually decides
- **A captured institution**: the city watch is corrupt and works for the smuggling ring
- **A power vacuum**: the king died, his heirs are arguing, the borderlands are governing themselves
- **A foreign hand**: the empire pretends this is a free province, but its consul has veto power
- **An invisible authority**: a god, a wizard, an immortal — never seen but obeyed
- **A succession crisis**: the rightful heir is a child, regents compete
- **A guild oligarchy**: officially a republic, actually run by five rich families

Pick one and make it specific. The crisis section often grows directly from this gap.

## Sanity checks before finalizing

Before declaring the dossier done, walk through this list. Anything that fails — fix or justify.

1. **Geography → ancestries**: Does every dominant ancestry fit the biome? If not, is there a one-line justification?
2. **Geography → religion**: Does the dominant deity make sense given the landscape? If not, why is this faith here?
3. **Geography → fauna**: Are the listed creatures plausible for the biome? If exotic, why?
4. **Factions orthogonality**: Pick any two factions — would their conflict be different from any other pair? If not, redesign.
5. **Politics specificity**: Is the active crisis concrete enough that PCs could step into it on session one?
6. **Hooks elements**: Does every hook reference at least one specific element from the dossier (deity, faction, location, creature)? If a hook is generic, rewrite to anchor it.
7. **Tone level fit**: At level 3, is the shadow/heretical deity present? Is one location "wrong"? Are at least 2 hooks morally costly? If level 3, but it feels like 1 or 5, recalibrate.
8. **Single-trait cultures**: Does any ancestry get described with a single adjective ("the warriors", "the merchants")? Add a contradiction.
9. **Specificity in fauna**: Did you write "bandits and wolves"? Replace with named, specific things.
10. **Off-stage handoffs**: Did you note which elements are ready for follow-up skills? If not, add them.

If you can answer yes to 1–7 and no to 8–9, and yes to 10, the dossier is coherent.
