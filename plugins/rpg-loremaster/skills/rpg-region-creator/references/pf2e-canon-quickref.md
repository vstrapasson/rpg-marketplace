# PF2e Canon Quick Reference (for region creation)

A curated index of Pathfinder 2e (post-Remaster) elements organized for picking-by-context, not for browsing. Use this when generating regions to choose deities, ancestries, creature families, and planar elements that fit the seed.

**Linking rule:** Always link to the AoN **category page**, never to a specific `?ID=` URL — IDs change and broken links are worse than no links. Examples:
- ✅ `[Pharasma](https://2e.aonprd.com/Deities.aspx)` — user searches within
- ❌ `[Pharasma](https://2e.aonprd.com/Deities.aspx?ID=14)` — may break

## Deities — by tone role

The 20 Core deities (Player Core / Remaster) organized by how they tend to function in a region. **For default level 3 generation, mix using roughly 5:3:2** — five elements heroic, three ambiguous, two shadowy.

### Heroic / civilizing pillars
Use as **dominant deity** for stable, virtuous regions; as **popular secondary** to give a region moral anchoring.

- **Iomedae** — Virtue, justice, valor. Knightly orders, paladins, ordered defense against evil.
- **Sarenrae** — Sun, healing, redemption (with sword). Healers, redemptive orders, desert sanctuaries.
- **Torag** — Protection, craftsmanship, dwarves. Fortified cities, smiths, lawful protectors.
- **Shelyn** — Beauty, art, love. Bards, artists, peaceful courts.
- **Erastil** — Community, hunting, family. Rural villages, frontier clans, hearth-faiths.
- **Abadar** — Civilization, commerce, law. Merchant cities, courts, banking.
- **Cayden Cailean** — Freedom, wine, courage. Taverns, liberators, "common folk" heroes.
- **Desna** — Dreams, travel, luck. Nomads, wanderers, dreamers.

### Ambiguous / textured pillars
Use as **dominant deity** for regions with weight and gravity; as **popular secondary** when you want layered piety.

- **Pharasma** — Death, birth, prophecy. Necropoleis, midwives, prophets — not evil, but heavy.
- **Gozreh** — Sea, wind, weather. Druids, sailors — indifferent rather than benevolent.
- **Gorum** — War, battle, glory. Warrior cultures, gladiators — violence as virtue.
- **Calistria** — Lust, vengeance, cunning. Courtesans, elves, the long memory of wrong.
- **Nethys** — Magic in all forms. Wizards, academies — obsession without ethics.
- **Norgorber** — Secrets, poison, assassins (the Four-Faced). Always somewhat shadowy.
- **Irori** — Self-perfection, knowledge. Monks, martial schools — disciplined but austere.

### Shadowy / antagonistic pillars
Use as **shadow / heretical third** at level 3+; as **dominant** only at levels 4–5; as cult focus or villain backdrop.

- **Asmodeus** — Tyranny, contracts, devils. Oppressive empires, infernal politics. Heavy presence in dark-leaning regions.
- **Urgathoa** — Undeath, disease, gluttony. Necromancers, vampires, cults of un-death.
- **Zon-Kuthon** — Pain, darkness, loss. Sadism ritualized, living shadows, gothic horror.
- **Lamashtu** — Monstrous birth, nightmares. Body horror, gnolls, monstrosities.
- **Rovagug** — Apocalyptic destruction. End-cults, cataclysm prophets.

### Non-Core options worth knowing
For ancestries with established panteons, use these (all on the [AoN Deities](https://2e.aonprd.com/Deities.aspx) page, filter by category):

- **Dwarven Gods** — Torag's pantheon (Bolka, Folgrit, Grundinnar, Kols, Magrim, Trudd)
- **Elven Deities** — Calistria's circle and other elven-aligned gods
- **The Eldest** — First World powers (fey-aligned, ambiguous)
- **Empyreal Lords** — semi-divine good powers (Heaven, Nirvana, Elysium)
- **Archdevils** — Hell's hierarchy (Asmodeus's lieutenants — great for political dark-leaning)
- **Demon Lords** — Abyss rulers (chaotic horror cults)
- **Horsemen of the Apocalypse** — Abaddon's princes (nihilistic apocalypse)
- **Great Old Ones / Outer Gods** — Cosmic horror (Yog-Sothoth, Hastur, Cthulhu exist in PF2e). Use as **distant** threats, not daily presence.

## Ancestries — by biome fit

The 8 Core ancestries from Player Core, with notes on where they plausibly thrive. Other ancestries exist on the [AoN Ancestries](https://2e.aonprd.com/Ancestries.aspx) page.

| Ancestry | Strong biome fit | Heritage detail to consider |
|---|---|---|
| **Human** | Everything | Always works — pick a heritage that matches the region (Skilled, Versatile, Wintertouched, Hopeful, Tide-Touched, etc.) |
| **Dwarf** | Mountains, hills, deep underground | Forge Dwarf for crafting cultures; Cavernous Dwarf for deep settlements; Anvil Dwarf for tough frontiers |
| **Elf** | Old forests, ancient ruins, magical sites | Whisper Elf (urban), Woodland Elf (forest), Cavern Elf (drow-adjacent), Seer Elf (mystic), Ancient Elf (multiclass) |
| **Gnome** | Forest edges, areas near First World, weird places | First World (fey-touched), Umbral (shadow-touched), Sensate (sensation-driven), Wellspring (magical) |
| **Goblin** | Wilderness, abandoned places, fringes | Charhide (fire-adapted), Snow Goblin (cold), Unbreakable, Razortooth |
| **Halfling** | Anywhere humans are (often integrated) | Hillock (rural), Twilight (urban-shadow), Nomadic (travelers), Gutsy |
| **Leshy** | Forests, jungles, magical groves | Cactus (desert), Fungus (cave/underground), Gourd, Leaf, Vine, Seapod |
| **Orc** | Wild frontiers, mountains, warbands | Badlands, Battle-Ready, Deep, Grave, Hold-Scarred, Rainfall, Winter |

### Useful versatile heritages (for tone and ancestry mixing)
- **Aiuvarin** — human + elf mixed heritage (any biome)
- **Dromaar** — human + orc mixed heritage (frontier regions)
- **Changeling** — child of a hag (good dark-leaning marker)
- **Dhampir** — vampire heritage (good dark-leaning marker)
- **Duskwalker** — soul that returned from the Boneyard (Pharasma-touched regions)
- **Nephilim** — planar bloodline; can be celestial-touched (angelkin) or fiend-touched (pitborn) — great for tone signaling
- **Mortic** — void-touched (very dark-leaning)
- **Beastkin** — lycanthrope-touched (folkloric horror regions)

## Bestiary families — by environment & role

Use the [AoN Monster Families](https://2e.aonprd.com/MonsterFamilies.aspx) page for the full list. Below is curated for region-building.

### By environment
- **Forest**: Fey, Hags, Lycanthropes, Treants, Wolves, Bears, Owlbears, Drakes, Ettercaps
- **Mountain**: Dragons, Giants, Griffons, Trolls, Yetis, Orcs, Stone elementals
- **Desert**: Mummies, Sphinxes, Genies (Efreeti), Sandwalkers, Couatl, Gnolls, Jackalweres
- **Swamp/Marsh**: Lizardfolk, Boggards, Hydras, Will-o'-Wisps, Hags (especially Green/Sea), Naga
- **Coast/Sea**: Sahuagin, Merfolk, Sea Devils, Sirens, Krakens, Ceratioidi
- **Underground**: Drow, Duergar, Mind Flayers (Devourers), Aberrations, Oozes, Xulgaths
- **Arctic**: Frost Giants, Winter Wolves, White Dragons, Yetis, Remorhazes, Frostmaul Giants
- **Urban**: Vampires, Wererats, Doppelgangers, Hauntings, Spies, Cultists, Devils (infiltrating)
- **Planar overlap**: Daemons, Demons, Devils, Azata, Archons, Aeons (where The Veil is thin)

### By role in a region
- **Apex threats (level 10+)**: Adult/Ancient Dragons, Liches, Demon Lords (cameo), Krakens, Tarrasque, Ancient Wyrms, Pit Fiends, Balors
- **Recurring antagonists (level 5–10)**: Vampires, Hags, Mid-tier Devils/Demons, Beholders (PF2e equivalent: Gauths), Giants, Drow nobles, Mummy Lords
- **Common threats (level 1–5)**: Bandits, Cultists, Goblins, Skeletons, Zombies, Wolves, Boars, Gnolls, Kobolds, Bugbears, Mid Spirits
- **Ambient creatures**: Animals (deer, hawk, bear, etc.), Sprites, Wisps, House spirits, Familiars-as-fauna

### For dark-leaning texture (always at least one)
- **Haunts** (PF2e has them as Hazards) — ghosts in specific places
- **Undead** (skeleton, zombie, ghoul, wight, wraith)
- **Aberrations** (xulgath, gug, neothelid — Lovecraftian)
- **Fiends** (especially Devils for political dark, Demons for chaotic horror)
- **Hags** (Green, Sea, Night — fairytale horror)
- **Lycanthropes** (body horror)

## Planes / cosmology — for sourcing supernatural elements

Use the [AoN Planes](https://2e.aonprd.com/Planes.aspx) page. Treat these as the **source** of any supernatural element in your region — a fiend is from Hell or the Abyss; a fey is from the First World; a haunting is leakage from the Ethereal Plane.

### Useful planes for region-building
- **Material Plane** — your region lives here
- **Shadow Plane** — best for "wrong" regions; overlays the Material, gothic horror, ideal for dark-leaning
- **First World** — fey origin; great for forests that are too-old, gnome heritages, weird beauty
- **Ethereal Plane** — ghosts, hauntings, lingering dead
- **Boneyard** — Pharasma's domain; necropoleis, judgment, psychopomps
- **Heaven** — archons, ordered good
- **Hell** — devils, contracts, lawful evil (great for politically corrupt dark-leaning)
- **The Abyss / Outer Rifts** — demons, chaotic evil (catastrophic dark)
- **Abaddon** — daemons, niihilism (apocalyptic dark)

### Pattern: every supernatural element traces to a plane
When you place a fiend, a fey, or an undead in your region, ask "why HERE?" — and the answer is almost always a planar link:
- A demon? → A rift to the Abyss, a cult that opened a portal, a binding gone wrong
- A fey court? → A First World incursion, an old pact, a ley-line
- A haunting? → Ethereal Plane leakage from violence, an unburied dead, a Pharasma-failed soul
- A devil network? → An old contract still in force, a noble's pact, an infiltration

This is the secret to making supernatural feel *grounded* rather than random.

## Languages — for cultural texture

The [AoN Languages](https://2e.aonprd.com/Languages.aspx) page has the full list. Useful patterns:

- **Common ancestry languages**: Common, Dwarven, Elven, Gnomish, Goblin, Halfling, Orcish, Sylvan
- **Planar languages** (mark cult presence or planar overlap): Celestial, Infernal, Abyssal, Daemonic, Aklo (Old Ones, very dark)
- **Regional human languages** (use to suggest cultural family without saying "the people here are basically X"): Hallit (frontier/north), Mwangi (jungle), Osiriani (desert), Polyglot (mixed), Taldane, Tien (eastern), Vudrani (southern)
- **Special**: Necril (undead — a clear dark-leaning marker), Thassilonian (ancient lost-empire vibes), Iobaric (frozen north)

## Hazards — for dark-leaning texture

Hazards are the **other half** of dark atmosphere — they make a place feel cursed without needing combat. See [AoN Hazards](https://2e.aonprd.com/Hazards.aspx).

### Types worth knowing for region work
- **Environmental hazards**: cave-in, quicksand, lava, blizzard — natural danger
- **Magical hazards**: summoning rune, spectral ambush, cursed altar — leftover wizardry
- **Haunts**: ghosts re-enacting trauma at a specific location — perfect for dark-leaning, no combat needed
- **Traps**: classic dungeon fare; less useful at region scale

In a level-3 region, including 1–2 hazards (often haunts) at specific named locations is a low-cost way to make the region *feel* heavy.
