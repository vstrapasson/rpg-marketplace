# Tone Spectrum — the 1–5 Dark-Leaning Scale

This file defines the five tone levels the skill can generate. Default is **level 3 (dark-leaning)**. Read this whenever you're generating at a level other than 3, or whenever you're unsure how much shadow to put into a region.

## The scale

| Level | Label | Reference works | What it feels like |
|---|---|---|---|
| **1** | Luminous classical fantasy | Forgotten Realms default, Dragonlance, classic D&D | Heroes shine, villains are clear, victory expected. Light dominates. |
| **2** | Heroic adventure | Pathfinder default, Reign of Winter, Eberron | Heroes function, world has real problems, victory likely. Light wins more often than not. |
| **3 ⭐ default** | **Dark-leaning** | Curse of Strahd, Wrath of the Righteous, Strange Aeons, Witcher 3 in general | Heroes possible but flawed; world has rotted patches between healthy ones; victory costs. Light and shadow alternate. |
| **4** | Dark fantasy | Berserk middle arcs, Reign of Winter late arcs, Hellknight Hill dark mode | Antiheroes common; world is mostly shadowy; victories are pyrrhic. Shadow dominates. |
| **5** | Grimdark | Berserk (Eclipse onward), Warhammer Fantasy, A Song of Ice and Fire | No heroes; world is lost; "victory" is survival. Shadow is total. |

## How each level changes the output

### Level 1 (Luminous classical)
- All factions have clean motivations
- Religion is straightforwardly comforting
- Threats are external (monsters from outside)
- Hooks are mostly "save the village / find the treasure / defeat the warlord"
- The dominant deity is from the heroic block (Iomedae, Sarenrae, Torag, Shelyn, Erastil)
- No shadow/heretical deity in religion section
- Fauna favors animals + classic monsters, not horror creatures

### Level 2 (Heroic adventure)
- One or two factions have moral complications
- Religion has a minor internal split (a heresy that's not dangerous, just doctrinal)
- Threats include some internal corruption (a corrupt magistrate, not a city-wide rot)
- 1–2 hooks involve ambiguity
- Mix of heroic + ambiguous deities (e.g., Iomedae + Pharasma)
- Fauna can include some undead or fey, but not horror-tier

### Level 3 ⭐ (Dark-leaning — DEFAULT)
- **The mix is 5:3:2** — five elements lean heroic/light, three lean ambiguous, two lean shadowy
- Religion features the full triple: 1 dominant (heroic or ambiguous) + 1 popular + 1 shadow/heretical (this last one always present at level 3+)
- At least one major faction has corruption inside it (not the whole faction — a faction inside the faction)
- The "active crisis" in politics has no clean answer
- At least 2 of the 5–7 hooks involve **moral cost** — you can't help A without hurting B
- At least one location is **wrong** in some way (a ruined temple, a haunted forest, a tainted spring)
- Sensory texture includes some unsettling notes (a tower that hums at night, a forest where birds don't sing in one specific glade)
- Fauna includes at least one creature with the `undead`, `fiend`, or `aberration` trait — but doesn't lead with it
- Heroes are absolutely possible, and the dominant deity often gives them an anchor

### Level 4 (Dark fantasy)
- The mix flips to 2:3:5 — two elements lean light, three ambiguous, five shadowy
- Religion: dominant deity is often ambiguous; the shadow presence is no longer hidden — it's politically active
- Most factions have rotted components
- The active crisis is dire; the obvious solutions are all bad
- Hooks routinely involve the lesser evil
- Sensory texture is overtly oppressive (the sun is dim, the wind is wrong, water tastes off)
- Fauna leads with horror traits (undead, fiend, aberration are featured, not background)
- Heroes feel rare and personal — they exist despite the world, not because of it
- The "off-stage notes" include things the user might want to consider — "this region wears people down; consider a session-zero conversation"

### Level 5 (Grimdark)
- The mix is 0:2:8 — no luminous elements at all
- Religion: dominant deity is openly malign or extinct; if heroic deities exist locally, they're persecuted
- Every faction is compromised
- The crisis is unsolvable — players choose who suffers, not whether anyone suffers
- Hooks are about survival, betrayal, doomed causes
- Sensory texture is unrelenting (ash falls, blood is in the soil, light itself feels diluted)
- Fauna is almost entirely horror-tier
- Heroism is performative — characters can act heroically, but the world will not reward it
- Add a content advisory in the off-stage notes — grimdark requires player buy-in

## How to apply level 3 specifically

When generating at level 3 (the default), use this practical checklist:

1. **In Religion**: always include the shadow/heretical third deity. Always.
2. **In Factions**: among 2–4 factions, exactly one should have an internal rot (a corrupt member, a hidden agenda, a compromised leader). The others should still be clean — that contrast is what makes the rot land.
3. **In Politics**: the active crisis must not have a morally clean answer. There should be at least three plausible actions, and each one costs something.
4. **In Locations**: among the 5–10 minor locations, at least 2 should be "wrong" — haunted, cursed, abandoned for unsettling reasons. Not all of them. A few.
5. **In Hooks**: at least 2 of the 5–7 hooks involve choosing between competing goods or between two bad options. Mark these hooks somehow in the output (e.g., italics on the dilemma).
6. **In Fauna**: at least one creature in the regular threats has a horror trait (undead, fiend, aberration, haunt). Apex threat doesn't have to be horror — it can be a noble dragon — but the texture has something off.
7. **In Sensory texture (Full version only)**: include one specifically unsettling sensory detail. One. Not five.

The goal of level 3 is **contrast**. Pure light feels naive; pure shadow feels exhausting. Dark-leaning works because the shadow is meaningful against the light, and the light is precious against the shadow.

## Common mistakes

- **Over-darkening.** If the user asked for level 3 and you wrote something that feels like 4 or 5, pull back. The test: could a paladin of Iomedae live here without compromising her oaths? At level 3, yes. At level 4, with effort. At level 5, no.
- **Under-darkening.** If the user asked for level 3 and you wrote something that feels like 1 or 2, the shadow presence is too quiet. Make the heretical deity active, make the cursed location matter, make at least one hook morally costly.
- **Sprinkling darkness without weight.** Don't add edgy details (skulls on spikes, blood in the wells) for shock value. Every shadow element should be *meaningful* — connected to a faction, a deity, a hook. Decorative darkness is the worst version of dark-leaning.

## Sliding between levels

If the user wants a level 3 region but says "go a little darker on the religion section", that's fine — you can mix. Generate at the base level overall but dial individual sections up or down by 1. Mark in the off-stage notes which sections are dialed away from the base level so the user knows.
