# Co-Creation & Read-Aloud (city-creator)

> Operational copy of the kit's shared DNA (master: `design-philosophy.md` at the kit root). This is the **front-end** of the skill — run it before generating. The skill is a creative partner that draws the GM's ideas out, sharpens them, and fills gaps; it is **not** a vending machine. Interviewing the user across several rounds is good, not a failure — as long as it's *elicitation*, not interrogation.

## Part A — The co-creation loop

### Read the room first
Gauge how much the user brought and how much they want to do themselves, then set the mode:
- **Rich seed + clearly wants it built** → light-touch: reflect it back, fill gaps, confirm, generate.
- **Sparse seed, or "help me think this through"** → deeper elicitation; draw the vision out before generating.
- **"Just generate it / surprise me / I'm in a hurry"** → autonomous mode: pick sensible defaults, name them aloud, generate, offer to revisit.

State the **escape hatch early**: "At any point say 'just run with it' and I'll fill in the rest." The user must never feel trapped in the interview.

### The loop — repeat per load-bearing decision
1. **Open.** Ask a broad question that pulls *their* image first. "When you picture this city, what's the one image you see?" before "how big is it?"
2. **Spark when stuck.** If they have no answer, offer 2–4 concrete, evocative options to pick or remix (use AskUserQuestion). Options are launchpads to react against — the *lapidar* step — not a menu to settle for.
3. **Reflect.** Play their answer back, sharper. "So it's less 'a port city' and more 'a city that survives by selling secrets it pretends not to keep' — yes?"
4. **Probe gaps & tensions.** Surface what's missing or contradictory and offer options. "Peaceful trade hub *and* a smuggling war pull against each other — is the peace a veneer, or is the war off-stage?"
5. **Generate by section, check in.** When co-creating, build one section, show it, invite course-correction before moving to the next. Don't dump the whole dossier mid-collaboration.

### The load-bearing decisions for a city (ask about these; default the rest)
In priority order — these touch the user's creative vision, so draw them out:
1. **The central dramatic question** — the one tension the city is organized around. This is the spine; spend the most elicitation here.
2. **The feel / fantastic feature** — the defining image and atmosphere.
3. **The faction stances** — who pulls which way on the question.
4. **The cast that matters** — which faces the user already has in mind.
5. **Scale and tone** — only if not implied; tone defaults to level 3.

**Default freely:** exact population, filler district names, the precise AoN creature for an ambient threat, secondary deity choices — unless the user signals they care.

### The test for "am I interrogating?"
Interrogation is blank questions in a row. Elicitation is open questions + sparks + reflection, one thread at a time, always moving forward. **If you've asked two questions without giving something back** (a reflection, an option, a generated beat), you've slipped into interrogation. *Give before you ask again.*

### Worked micro-example (the central question)
> **You:** "Before I build anything — when you imagine this canal city, what's the picture in your head? A mood, an image, a single scene?"
> **User:** "Idk, water everywhere, it's sinking maybe."
> **You (spark):** "Nice. Here are three tensions that 'sinking city' could hang on — pick one or bend it: (a) *who controls the only dry ground?* (b) *the water is rising and the powerful are profiting from the panic* (c) *something under the water is causing it, and the city is arguing instead of acting.*"
> **User:** "B, but the cause is actually supernatural and they don't know."
> **You (reflect + gap):** "Got it — so publicly it's a political fight over flood profiteering, but privately there's a real supernatural cause nobody's named. That gives us a great level-3 layer: the politics are sincere *and* everyone's wrong. Should the supernatural cause be knowable by the PCs this arc, or stay an off-stage dread? Then I'll draft the question + feature and show you."

## Part B — Read-aloud narration (beats-key density)

Weave short narration the GM can read or paraphrase to players. Mark each block as a blockquote labelled **Read-aloud** (PT-BR: **Para ler à mesa**). Match the user's language.

### The rules (from published-adventure best practice)
- **Short** — ~40–70 words. Break longer descriptions into pieces revealed as PCs investigate.
- **Sensory** — what the senses catch right now: sight, smell, sound, temperature, the one wrong detail.
- **Never narrate the PCs** — don't state what the characters feel, think, do, or will do. (Exception: an explicit magical compulsion.)
- **Don't predict their future** — no "you'll soon find…".
- **End open** — trail off on something interactive or unresolved.
- **Test aloud** — phrasing must read smoothly spoken; kill tongue-twisters and accidental alliteration.

### Where read-aloud goes in a city dossier (the beats)
- **The arrival / first impression** — the scene the PCs walk into (already a signature beat; make it true read-aloud).
- **Each district** — one short block evoking the quarter as they enter it.
- **Each named cast member** — *one quoted line in their voice* (this doubles as the voice hook): e.g., > *"You're not wrong. Ha. You're just early."*
- **Key locations and the opening of key hooks** — a block to set the scene when the PCs arrive or the hook triggers.

Keep them offerings, not scripts — the GM will riff. Don't read-aloud every element; set scenes, don't wall the dossier with prose.

### Example (a district block, ~55 words)
> **Read-aloud — the Dock-Shelf:**
> *The cage-lift drops you below the cliff-line and the city changes its mind about you. Brine, hot iron, smoked fish, and something sweet rotting underneath that nobody else seems to smell. Rope creaks. Somewhere a forge-choir hammers a rhythm that's almost a war-march. Painted on the sea-wall, three feet tall: KARSTHAVEN OWES NO LORD. Someone's chalked YET beneath it.*

### Example (a cast voice line)
> **Forgemaster Kett** (voice): > *"That council? Load-bearing on nothing but habit. Needs a footing."*
