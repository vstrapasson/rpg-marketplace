# PF2e Canon Quick Reference (for city creation)

A curated index of Pathfinder 2e (post-Remaster) elements organized **for urban play** — deities by what they DO in a city, ancestry mixing in settlements, the "enemy within" bestiary, NPC stat-block anchors, and archetype roles for casting. Use this to pick elements that fit a settlement, not to browse.

## 1. Deities by urban function

Cities concentrate and politicize faith. Pick deities by **what their faith DOES in a settlement**, not by cosmology. For default tone 3, mix roughly **5:3:2** — five elements heroic/civic, three ambiguous, two shadowy. Full list: [AoN Deities](https://2e.aonprd.com/Deities.aspx).

### Runs the institutions (civic faiths)
- **Abadar** — banks, courts, mints, contracts, the merchant council. The default "establishment" faith of any trade city. His temple often *is* the treasury.
- **Iomedae** — the cathedral, the knightly order, the city guard's chaplaincy. Order and lawful defense; can shade into self-righteous enforcement.
- **Sarenrae** — hospitals, hospices, redemption missions, the dawn-bell. Healing and second chances; her clergy run the poorhouses.
- **Shelyn** — opera houses, art guilds, the marriage-registry, festivals. Beauty as civic pride.
- **Torag** — the forge district, the walls, the engineers' guild, dwarven quarters. Protection and craft.

### Runs the streets (folk / ambiguous faiths)
- **Cayden Cailean** — taverns, dockworker fraternities, the "common folk" who distrust the council. Liberty and drink; foments riots.
- **Calistria** — courtesan houses, information brokers, the long memory of slights. Vengeance and cunning; elven quarters.
- **Pharasma** — the necropolis, the morgue, the midwives, the plague-watch. Not evil, but heavy; her clergy decide who's buried and who's burned.
- **Desna** — caravanserais, the traveler's shrine, immigrants and refugees. Luck and the road.
- **Gozreh** — the harbor, the fishing fleet, the weather-priests. Indifferent power the docks must appease.

### Runs the underworld (shadow presences — tone 3+ always has one)
- **Norgorber** — the thieves' guild, the poisoners, the spy network, the assassins. The patron saint of every city's hidden half. Four faces (secrets, greed, poison, murder) — pick one for a cult.
- **Asmodeus** — the loan sharks with infernal paper, the corrupt magistrate, the noble house with a contract. Tyranny dressed as law; perfect for political dark-leaning.
- **Urgathoa** — gluttony cults among the decadent rich, plague-bringers, the undertaker who sells corpses. Disease and undeath beneath the gilding.
- **Zon-Kuthon** — a hidden sadist-cult, a pain-temple in the undercity, shadows that move wrong. Gothic horror in the cellars.
- **Mammon / archdevils & demon lords** — for a city with a deeper infernal or chaotic rot; use as a cult's true patron. See [AoN Deities](https://2e.aonprd.com/Deities.aspx) (filter by category).

### Quick picks by city concept
| City concept | Civic | Street | Shadow |
|---|---|---|---|
| Trade hub / free port | Abadar | Cayden, Gozreh | Norgorber |
| Holy city / cathedral seat | Iomedae or Sarenrae | Shelyn | a heresy of the civic faith |
| Necropolis / plague city | Pharasma | Pharasma (folk) | Urgathoa |
| Decadent noble capital | Abadar, Shelyn | Calistria | Asmodeus |
| Frontier boomtown | Cayden, Erastil | Desna | Lamashtu cult |

## 2. Ancestries in cities

Cities **mix freely** — unlike a region, biome doesn't gate who lives here; *trade, refuge, and history* bring everyone. The interesting axis in a city isn't "which ancestry fits the climate" but **which ancestry holds which quarter, trade, or class** — and the friction between them.

- **Humans** — everywhere; pick heritages matching the city's culture.
- **Dwarves** — the forge quarter, the engineers, the bank vaults, the stubborn old guild that won't modernize.
- **Elves** — the old families with long grudges (Calistria), the artists, the "they were here before the walls" minority.
- **Gnomes** — the alchemists, the strange-shop district, the underclass of tinkers; bleachling risk gives them a dark edge.
- **Halflings** — integrated underclass: servants, dockhands, the best cooks and the best thieves; often run the real economy from below.
- **Goblins / Orcs** — recent arrivals, refugee blocks, the press-ganged labor, the feared edge of town. Friction-rich.
- **Leshy / unusual ancestries** — a single notable resident or a tiny enclave; a hook in themselves.

### Versatile heritages — urban dark-leaning markers
Cities are where planar and cursed bloodlines blend in. Great for the cast and for shadow texture (all on [AoN Ancestries](https://2e.aonprd.com/Ancestries.aspx)):
- **Nephilim** — celestial-touched (angelkin) or fiend-touched (pitborn); a noble house with infernal blood is a faction in itself.
- **Dhampir** — the pale patron who's only seen at night; the vampire's get.
- **Changeling** — a hag's daughter hiding in plain sight among the gentry.
- **Duskwalker** — Pharasma-marked; the gravedigger who came back, the plague-survivor.
- **Aiuvarin / Dromaar** — mixed-heritage commoners; the lived reality of a port that mixes everyone.

**Casting tip:** give at least one cast member a versatile heritage tied to a faction or the shadow faith — it wires ancestry into the drama instead of leaving it as flavor.

## 3. The "enemy within" — urban bestiary

City threats favor things that **hide among people** over wilderness megafauna. The horror of a city is that the monster has a name on the council rolls. Use [AoN Creatures](https://2e.aonprd.com/Creatures.aspx) and [AoN Monster Families](https://2e.aonprd.com/MonsterFamilies.aspx).

### Infiltrators (the signature urban menace)
- **Doppelgangers** — replaced a cast member; who is real?
- **Wererats** — the thieves' guild *is* a were-rat clan; the undercity is theirs.
- **Vampires & spawn** — the reclusive patron, the noble who hosts only night-salons.
- **Devils (infiltrating)** — contract-bound, working through a magistrate or a banking house.
- **Hags (urban: covens posing as crones, midwives, fortune-tellers)** — the wise-woman everyone trusts.
- **Mimics / faceless stalkers** — rarer, for a specific haunted building.

### Organized mortal threats
- **Thieves' rings, smuggler crews, cultists, corrupt watch, gangs, spies, assassins (Norgorber).** These are *factions* as much as monsters — often better handled in the faction web.

### Undead & the rot beneath
- **Ghouls / graveknights in the catacombs, a wraith in the old quarter, skeletal labor raised illegally, a plague of the risen dead.** Necropolis and plague cities lead with these.

### Constructs & the made
- **Golems, homunculi, animated guardians, a clockwork left running by a dead wizard.** For mage-cities and ruined high-magic districts.

### Apex / patron-tier (one, optional)
- A **vampire lord, a pit fiend working a long contract, a lich beneath the library, a coven matriarch, an aboleth in the cisterns.** Defines the danger ceiling — often the secret answer to "who *really* runs this city?"

### Tone-3 rule
At least one urban threat carries a horror trait (`undead`, `fiend`, `aberration`), but the city doesn't *lead* with horror — it leads with people. The monster is the twist under the politics.

## 4. NPC stat-block anchors (don't invent numbers)

This skill sketches **personality and role**, not stats. When the GM needs mechanics, point them at a **reusable PF2e NPC stat block** rather than building one. PF2e ships a large gallery of generic NPCs (guard, cutpurse, noble, cultist, spy, mage, crime boss, etc.) usable as-is or reskinned. Find them on [AoN Creatures](https://2e.aonprd.com/Creatures.aspx) (search the NPC name) — common anchors:

| Cast role | Reusable PF2e NPC block to suggest |
|---|---|
| City guard / watch | Guard, Watch Officer, Sergeant |
| Street thief / fixer | Cutpurse, Thief, Bandit |
| Crime boss | Crime Lord, Bandit Lord |
| Noble / council member | Noble, Aristocrat |
| Priest / zealot | Acolyte, Cultist, Cult Leader |
| Spy / informant | Spy, Charlatan |
| Hired sword / bodyguard | Mercenary, Bodyguard, Knight |
| Mage patron | Adept, Mage, Necromancer |
| Common petitioner | Commoner, Beggar, Merchant |

Tell the user: "Use the **[role]** stat block from Monster Core / the NPC Gallery as-is, or bump its level to fit your party." Don't write attack bonuses or HP yourself.

## 5. NPC archetype roles (for casting)

A starting palette for the 4–6 named cast. Pick a spread, tie each to the central question or a faction, and **subvert at least one**.

- **The power broker** — holds the real authority behind the throne (often the de facto ruler).
- **The fixer** — can get you anything, for a price and a favor owed.
- **The desperate petitioner** — needs the PCs' help, can't pay much, and is hiding something.
- **The rival** — wants the same thing the PCs will want; not evil, just first.
- **The informant** — sells truth in pieces; the Alexandrian "node" that points to other nodes.
- **The zealot** — true-believer for the civic or shadow faith; will not bend.
- **The merchant prince** — wealth as power; the orthogonal-faction anchor.
- **The outcast who knows** — the only one who'll tell the PCs what's really going on, and nobody believes them.
- **The face of the rot** — at tone 3, the one charming, respectable figure who's the internal corruption.

**Subversion examples:** the kindly innkeeper who informs for the watch; the crime boss who's the only one feeding the slums; the inquisitor who's right; the beggar who's the deposed heir.

## 6. Hazards & haunts for urban dread

The other half of dark atmosphere — makes a place feel cursed without combat. See [AoN Hazards](https://2e.aonprd.com/Hazards.aspx).
- **Haunts** — a ghost re-enacting a murder at a specific address; the bridge where the drowned call; the cell that whispers. Perfect for tone 3, no fight required.
- **Environmental** — rotten scaffolding, sewer gas, a collapsing tenement, river-flood.
- **Magical** — a cursed fountain, a ward gone sour, a summoning circle in a cellar, a noble's paranoid traps.

In a tone-3 city, one named haunt at a specific address is a cheap, strong way to make a quarter feel wrong.

## Anti-pattern reminder

A city is **people and tension**, not geography or a service directory. If you're reaching into this file for "what fish live in the harbor" or "what's the average price of an inn room", you're building a gazetteer — stop and reach instead for deities-as-institutions, the enemy-within roster, and the archetype roles. Those are what a GM points at during play.
