# Urban Coherence Rules

A **region** coheres around geography — everything cascades from the land. A **city** coheres around its **central question and faction web** — the social tension is the bedrock, and every element must connect to it. This file covers: settlement scale, district archetypes, faction orthogonality for cities, the central-question test, and the finalizing checklist.

## The causal chain (city version)

Build outward from the central question, not from geography.

```
Central question (the one tension)
    ↓
Faction web (who answers it differently, and how)
    ↓
The cast (the faces of those factions + the question)
    ↓
Powers / faith (where official and real authority sit)
    ↓
Districts (where the factions and question are PLACED)
    ↓
Threats (what hides among the people)
    ↓
Hooks (what a PC can do about the question today)
```

The test for **every** element: *does this connect to the central question or to a faction?* If a district, temple, NPC, or threat connects to nothing, it's decoration — cut it or wire it in.

## Settlement scale

Scale sets faction count, district count, cast size, and the kind of trouble. Population bands follow PF2e/d20 settlement conventions — use as a feel, not a census.

| Scale | Rough population | Factions | Districts | Cast (named) | Feels like |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Thorp** | 20–80 | 1–2 (often just families) | 0–1 (it's one place) | 2–3 | A crossroads, a single inn, one secret |
| **Village** | 100–600 | 2 | 1–2 | 3–4 | Everyone knows everyone; one outsider changes things |
| **Town** | 600–3,000 | 2–3 | 2–3 | 4 | A market, a temple, a lord; real but knowable |
| **City** ⭐ default | 5,000–25,000 | 3–4 | 3–5 | 4–6 | Quarters with their own character; anonymity possible |
| **Metropolis** | 25,000+ | 4 (+sub-factions) | 4–6 | 5–6 | A world unto itself; the PCs can't know it all |

**Scaling notes:**
- Below **town**, the "faction web" may be families, cliques, or a single tension between two people — keep the *shape* (orthogonal stances on one question) even when small.
- For **metropolis**, don't try to cover everything — pick the *one district* and *one question* the campaign is actually about and detail those; gesture at the rest. (Shea's "build around the PCs", Alexandrian's "zoom to the active nodes".)
- More factions ≠ better. Three orthogonal factions generate more play than six muddy ones.

## District archetypes

A palette for the **light** district spine. Each district in the output gets *one line + what faction/question lives there* — these archetypes are starting points, not a checklist to fill. Pick the 3–5 that the central question actually touches.

- **The Market / Mercantile** — guilds, the merchant council, Abadar's temple, the contested trade.
- **The High Quarter / Noble** — old families, the de jure power, the gilded rot.
- **The Docks / Wharf / Caravanserai** — smuggling, Gozreh, immigrants, the city's lungs and its underbelly.
- **The Temple District / Cathedral Close** — the civic faith, pilgrims, the political clergy.
- **The Undercity / Slums / Warrens** — the working poor, the thieves' guild, the plague, the shadow faith. Where tone-3 darkness lives.
- **The Craft Quarter / Forge** — dwarves, engineers, alchemists, the smoke.
- **The Civic / Government Core** — the council hall, the courts, the watch HQ, the gallows.
- **The Foreign Quarter / Refuge** — recent arrivals, a minority ancestry, Desna, friction.
- **The Ruin / Old Town / Drowned Quarter** — what the city was built over; the fantastic feature often lives here.

**Rule:** a district earns its place only if a faction, the question, or a cast member is *anchored* there. A district with nothing in it is map decoration — drop it.

## Faction orthogonality (city version)

The biggest faction failure is binary structure — "good guys" vs "bad guys". Every city should pass this test:

**For any pair of factions A and B, their conflict should differ from any pair (A, C) or (B, C).**

If A clashes with B for the same reason A clashes with C, you have one protagonist and two enemies — flat. In a city, the cleanest way to get orthogonality is to give each faction a **different stance on the central question** plus a **different kind of leverage**.

✅ **Good** (central question: *who controls the rising floodwater?*):
- **The Lockwardens' Guild** — engineers who own the floodgates; want to *sell* water access by the barrel.
- **The Dockside Commons** — flooded poor; want the gates opened and the guild broken.
- **The Pharasmin Chapter** — see the flood as judgment; want the drowned quarter abandoned to the dead.
- **House Verrin** (noble) — want to buy the gates and corner the city's water as a monopoly.

→ Guild vs Commons: pay or drown. Guild vs House: who owns the gates. Commons vs Pharasmins: abandon the quarter or save it. House vs Pharasmins: profit vs piety. Every pair fights about something different. A PC can side with anyone and make new conflict.

❌ **Bad:**
- The Honest Watch (heroes), the Crime Syndicate (villains), the Cult (also villains) → all conflicts collapse to "heroes vs wrong people." One direction only.

When you generate factions, **write the pairwise conflicts out**. If two read the same, redesign one.

## The central-question test

Before building anything, validate the question:
1. **One sentence, no obvious answer.** If there's a clearly-right resolution, it's not a question, it's a quest.
2. **At least three factions answer it differently** — and each answer benefits them. If you can't name three stances, the question is too thin.
3. **A PC can act on it on session one.** If it's purely background ("the city has always been corrupt"), sharpen it to a flashpoint ("the corruption trial starts Friday and witnesses are vanishing").
4. **It resonates with the fantastic feature** (ideally). The flooded city + the rising-water question; the dead-god city + the question of who inherits its power.

## Politics: de jure vs. de facto

The gap between official and real authority is the cheapest urban dark-leaning texture. Pick one:
- **Puppet council** — they vote; one banker holds all their debts.
- **Captured watch** — the law works for the smugglers.
- **Power vacuum** — the lord died, heirs squabble, the guilds govern by default.
- **Foreign hand** — a "free city" with an imperial consul holding veto.
- **Invisible authority** — a vampire patron, a wizard, an immortal nobody sees but everyone obeys (often the apex threat).
- **Guild oligarchy** — officially a republic, actually five families.

The active crisis usually grows straight from this gap.

## Sanity checks before finalizing

Walk this list. Anything that fails — fix or wire it in.

1. **Central question is stateable in one sentence** with no obvious answer. If vague, sharpen.
2. **Every faction is a distinct stance on the question** with distinct leverage. If two overlap, merge or redesign.
3. **Faction orthogonality:** pairwise conflicts all differ. If two read the same, redesign one.
4. **Every cast member connects** to a faction or the question. No decoration NPCs.
5. **Cast has voice hooks** — each named NPC has something an improvising GM can grab. If one is a flat quest-dispenser, add a trait or contradiction.
6. **De jure ≠ de facto** is named.
7. **Active crisis is steppable on session one** — concrete, current, not "someday."
8. **Tone-3 markers present:** shadow faith included; exactly one faction has internal rot; at least one threat carries a horror trait; at least 2 hooks carry moral cost; at least one location is "wrong."
9. **Districts are thin and anchored** — one line each, each holding a faction/question element. If a district holds nothing, cut it. If you wrote shop inventories or prices, trim to "what's contested/scarce."
10. **Threats hide among people** where possible, and connect to the drama (the doppelganger is impersonating a cast member, not roaming an alley at random).
11. **Every hook references a specific generated element.** If a hook could be transplanted to any city, anchor it.
12. **Off-stage handoffs are specific** — named items ready for npc-/faction-/location- skills; region facts honored.
13. **Not a gazetteer.** Read the whole thing: does it read like people-and-tension, or like a tourist guide? If the latter, cut description and add faces and stakes.
14. **Not a re-run of the region.** Is the bulk the social network (factions, cast, question)? Or did you rebuild geography/ancestries-by-biome/cosmology? If the latter, the city has no distinct value — shift weight to the social layer.

If you can answer yes to 1–13 and the city passes 14, it coheres.
