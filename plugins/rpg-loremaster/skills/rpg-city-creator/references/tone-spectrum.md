# Tone Spectrum — the 1–5 Dark-Leaning Scale (city edition)

Defines the five tone levels this skill can generate. Default is **level 3 (dark-leaning)**. Read this whenever generating at a level other than 3, or whenever unsure how much shadow to put into a city. (This mirrors the region-creator's scale, reframed for urban elements — factions, cast, districts, faith.)

## The scale

| Level | Label | Reference works | What it feels like |
|---|---|---|---|
| **1** | Luminous classical | Forgotten Realms default, Dragonlance | Heroes shine, villains are clear, victory expected. Light dominates. |
| **2** | Heroic adventure | Pathfinder default, Eberron | Heroes function, the city has real problems, victory likely. |
| **3 ⭐ default** | **Dark-leaning** | Curse of Strahd (Barovia/Vallaki), Wrath of the Righteous (Kenabres), Witcher 3 (Novigrad/Oxenfurt) | Heroes possible but flawed; gilded quarters beside rotted ones; victory costs. Light and shadow alternate. |
| **4** | Dark fantasy | Berserk middle arcs, Witcher books darker | Antiheroes common; the city is mostly shadow; victories are pyrrhic. |
| **5** | Grimdark | Berserk (Eclipse onward), Warhammer Fantasy cities, ASOIAF (King's Landing at its worst) | No heroes; the city is lost; "victory" is survival. |

## How each level changes the city

### Level 1 (Luminous classical)
- All factions have clean motivations; the conflict is "who serves the city best."
- Faith is straightforwardly comforting; no shadow presence.
- Threats are external or clearly villainous (a monster in the sewers, a foreign spy).
- Hooks are "save the merchant / catch the thief / defend the gate."
- Civic deity from the heroic block (Iomedae, Sarenrae, Abadar, Shelyn).

### Level 2 (Heroic adventure)
- One or two factions have moral complications.
- Faith has a minor split (a doctrinal heresy, not a dangerous cult).
- Some internal corruption (a bent magistrate), not city-wide rot.
- 1–2 hooks involve ambiguity.
- Mix of heroic + ambiguous deities.

### Level 3 ⭐ (Dark-leaning — DEFAULT)
- **Mix 5:3:2** — five elements lean heroic/civic, three ambiguous, two shadowy.
- **Faith:** the full triple — civic + street + shadow. The shadow presence (a cult, a banned rite, a whispered chapel) is **always** present at level 3+.
- **Factions:** exactly **one** of the 2–4 factions has an internal rot (a corrupt lieutenant, a secret patron, a compromised ideal). The others stay clean — that contrast is what makes the rot land.
- **Powers:** the active crisis (the central question at flashpoint) has **no clean answer** — at least three plausible actions, each costing something.
- **Districts:** at least one quarter is "wrong" — a drowned slum, a plague block, a noble close that's too quiet.
- **Threats:** at least one carries a horror trait (`undead`, `fiend`, `aberration`) — but the city leads with people, not monsters. The monster is the twist under the politics (the respected patron is a vampire; the doppelganger sits on the council).
- **Hooks:** at least 2 of 5–7 involve **moral cost** — help A and you hurt B.
- Heroes are absolutely possible; the civic faith often gives them an anchor.

### Level 4 (Dark fantasy)
- Mix flips to 2:3:5.
- Faith: the civic deity is ambiguous or hollow; the shadow presence is openly powerful (the Asmodean bank *is* the government).
- Most factions have rotted components; clean actors are rare and vulnerable.
- The crisis is dire; every obvious solution is bad.
- Hooks routinely involve the lesser evil.
- Threats lead with horror; the enemy-within is the norm.
- Heroes exist *despite* the city, not because of it. Consider a session-zero note.

### Level 5 (Grimdark)
- Mix 0:2:8 — no luminous elements.
- Faith: the dominant power is openly malign or the gods have abandoned the city.
- Every faction is compromised; the question is "who suffers", not "whether anyone suffers."
- Hooks are survival, betrayal, doomed causes.
- The city itself is the antagonist.
- Add a content advisory in the off-stage notes — grimdark needs player buy-in.

## Applying level 3 specifically (the default checklist)

1. **Faith:** always include the shadow third. Always.
2. **Factions:** exactly one carries internal rot; the rest stay clean for contrast.
3. **Powers:** the active crisis has no morally clean answer (≥3 costed actions).
4. **Districts:** at least one quarter is "wrong" in a specific, named way.
5. **Threats:** at least one horror-trait creature — as a *twist*, not the headline.
6. **Hooks:** at least 2 of 5–7 carry a moral cost; mark them (italics on the dilemma line).
7. **Cast:** at least one charming, respectable face is the rot (the "face of the rot" archetype).
8. **Sensory (Full only):** one specifically unsettling detail. One — not five.

The goal of level 3 is **contrast**. Pure light feels naive; pure shadow feels exhausting. Dark-leaning works because the shadow is meaningful against the light, and the light is precious against the shadow.

## Common mistakes

- **Over-darkening.** Test: *could a paladin of Iomedae live and serve in this city without breaking her oaths?* At level 3, yes. At level 5, no. If you wrote a 4 when asked for a 3, pull back.
- **Under-darkening.** If it reads like a 1–2, the shadow is too quiet: make the cult active, make one quarter genuinely wrong, make the respectable patron a monster, make 2 hooks hurt.
- **Decorative darkness.** Don't sprinkle skulls and blood for shock. Every shadow element must connect to a faction, a deity, a cast member, or a hook. Decorative darkness is the worst dark-leaning.

## Sliding between levels

If the user wants a level-3 city but "darker on the undercity," generate at base 3 and dial individual sections up or down by one. Note in the off-stage section which sections deviate from the base, so downstream skills keep consistency.
