# Location Frameworks — the design engine

The toolkit for building a location as a *place that makes sense*. Load for every location. Grounded in Gygaxian naturalism / "living dungeons" (Sly Flourish), Xandering the dungeon (Justin Alexander, after Jennell Jaquays), and the Five Room Dungeon (Johnn Four).

## 1. Premise & ecology — the bedrock

A location is not a set of rooms; it's a *place with a history that runs without the PCs.* Build it in four moves:

- **What it was** — original function. (A temple to Pharasma; a silver mine; a border fort; a noble's summer manor.)
- **What happened** — the turn. (A massacre; a flood; a summoning; a slow abandonment; a curse.)
- **What it is now** — current state + who occupies it.
- **The ecology** — how the current inhabitants *live*: what they eat, where they sleep, who patrols, who rules, who feuds. Sly Flourish's "living dungeon" test: could you describe a normal day here with no PCs present? If not, it's a level, not a place.

The ecology is what makes a location feel real and gives the PCs things to *exploit* — a predator the inhabitants fear, a rival faction in the lower levels, a captive who'll guide them, a resource the monsters need.

## 2. "Why hasn't it been cleared?" — the mandatory question

The signature of this skill. Any location with treasure or danger that's sat untouched near civilization needs a reason, or immersion breaks. Pick (or combine) one:

| Pattern | The reason it's untouched |
|---|---|
| **The guardian** | something powerful holds it — a dragon, a bound elemental, a lich; locals know better |
| **The curse** | those who enter don't return, or return *wrong* |
| **The taboo** | a religious/cultural prohibition; the locals *could* but *won't* |
| **The returner** | it's been cleared before — and whatever's there comes back / regrows |
| **The price** | the way in costs something (a toll of blood, a sacrifice, a one-way door) |
| **The hidden** | nobody knows it's here, or the entrance is lost/sealed/underwater |
| **The owner** | a powerful person/faction forbids entry and enforces it |
| **The recent** | it *just* became accessible (an earthquake opened it, the owner just died, the flood receded) |

Whichever you pick, it should ripple into the ecology and the hooks. "The recent" is the cleanest for a brand-new accessible site; "the returner" and "the curse" carry the most dark-leaning weight.

## 3. The five-room skeleton (Compact pacing)

A lean, complete beat sequence — combat, exploration, and social in one short session (Johnn Four). Use for Compact. The five beats are *functional roles*, not literally five rooms:

1. **Entrance & guardian** — sets mood and stakes; a threshold challenge (a sentinel, a locked rite, a hazard) that says "you are leaving the safe world."
2. **Puzzle or roleplay challenge** — a change of pace; something to solve or someone to parley with (a trapped survivor, a rival party, a riddle-warden).
3. **Trick or setback** — raises difficulty or costs the PCs something (a betrayal, a collapse, a drain, a false victory).
4. **Climax** — the big confrontation; reveals the truth and asks the most of the party.
5. **Reward, revelation, or twist** — treasure, a secret laid bare, or a hook into the next adventure.

Even a 3-area Compact site maps to these beats (some beats share an area). The sequence guarantees pacing without a full map.

## 4. Xandering the space (Full layout)

For Full, lay the space out *non-linearly* so exploration is a series of meaningful choices (Justin Alexander, after Jennell Jaquays). A linear corridor of rooms is the failure mode. Techniques:

- **Multiple entrances** — at least two ways in (the front gate *and* the collapsed cellar / the sea-cave / the chimney). Each entrance frames the dungeon differently.
- **Loops** — paths that connect back, so the PCs can circle around rather than backtrack a dead-end corridor. Avoid the "string of pearls."
- **Vertical connections** — shafts, stairs, chasms, lifts linking non-adjacent levels (a pit in Level I that drops to Level III). Verticality multiplies route choices cheaply.
- **Midpoint entry** — let the PCs enter in the *middle* of the structure, with the place extending above *and* below them, so the first decision is "up or down?"
- **Sub-level / secret branch** — an optional area reachable only by a discovered route, holding the deepest secret or treasure.

Render it as a numbered **area key** plus an **area-connection list** (or a simple text/ASCII sketch) so the GM can map it: e.g., `A1 ↔ A2 ↔ A3; A2 ↘ A5 (shaft); A5 ↔ A4 ↔ A1 (loop); A6 secret, from A4`. Every area should earn its place in the ecology — not "monster + treasure," but "the flooded gallery where the ghouls can't follow," "the shrine the cult still tends."

## 5. The dynamic element — the place isn't static

A location should change if the PCs linger or act — lighter than a faction's clock, but alive. Pick one or two:
- **A timer** — a ritual nearing completion, a tide rising to flood lower areas, a structure collapsing, dawn/dusk changing what's active.
- **A reaction** — the dominant inhabitants notice intrusion and regroup, fortify, flee, or hunt.
- **A shifting space** — doors that relock, a maze that rearranges, water that rises behind the party.

In Full, you may render the timer as a short clock (e.g., "4 ticks: the choir finishes the hymn → the seal cracks → the bound thing wakes → it walks"). Keep it to one or two moving parts; the location is a place, not a faction.

## 6. Hazards, treasure & secrets

- **Hazards & haunts** are the other half of danger — they make a place lethal *without* combat and carry tone cheaply. Environmental (cave-in, flood, bad air, lava), magical (cursed altar, summoning rune, ward gone sour), and **haunts** (a ghost re-enacting trauma at a specific spot — perfect for tone 3, no fight required). At tone 3+, include at least one haunt or a "wrong" area. Link [AoN Hazards](https://2e.aonprd.com/Hazards.aspx).
- **Treasure** ties to the premise: a tomb holds grave-goods and a signet; a guild vault holds coin, ledgers, and blackmail; a wizard's tower holds scrolls and a dangerous experiment. Avoid the loot piñata — make at least one prize *cost* something or *mean* something (cursed, contested, sacred, evidence).
- **The secret** rewards investigation: a false wall, a survivor who knows the truth, the real reason the place fell, a second exit, the identity of the guardian. It should change how the PCs understand the location.

## 7. Location archetypes (nine types)

Starting frames — pick one, subvert one expectation. Each: typical premise, ecology, a why-not-cleared lean, and a dark-leaning angle.

| Type | Was / is now | Ecology | Why not cleared | Dark-leaning angle |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Dungeon (built complex)** | a fortress/prison/temple, now occupied | a faction holds it; rivals in the depths | guardian / owner | the prisoners were never freed |
| **Lair (creature den)** | a natural den a monster claimed | apex predator + prey + scavengers | the guardian itself | the "monster" is defending young / is sapient |
| **Ruin (fallen structure)** | a grand place, now broken | scavengers, squatters, what killed it | the returner / curse | the disaster is recurring, not past |
| **Crypt / tomb** | a burial site | undead, grave-robbers, wards | taboo / curse | the dead were buried *to keep them in* |
| **Temple (sacred site)** | a place of worship | a cult, or what the faith contained | taboo / the price | the religion that turned; what they worshipped |
| **Tower (mage/watch)** | a wizard's or sentinel's tower | constructs, experiments, wards | owner / hidden | the experiment that's still running |
| **Cave system (natural)** | natural caverns | layered ecology by depth; deep things | hidden / the price | what lives where light never reached |
| **Manor / estate** | a noble's home | servants/family gone wrong, a haunting | owner / curse | the genteel rot; the family secret |
| **Landmark (open site)** | a monument, henge, battlefield, bridge | what gathers there; pilgrims/predators | taboo / returner | the site remembers; the haunt of the dead |

## Quick build checklist
1. Premise complete (was / happened / is now) and **"why not cleared?" answered**?
2. Ecology passes the "normal day with no PCs" test?
3. Fantastic feature stated (one striking image)?
4. Space is non-linear (Full: ≥2 entrances + a loop + a vertical link) or a clean five-room sequence (Compact)?
5. Dominant inhabitant + a secondary/rival presence (a wedge to exploit)?
6. At least one hazard/haunt (tone 3: a "wrong" area)?
7. Treasure ties to the premise; at least one prize costs or means something?
8. A secret that rewards investigation?
9. A dynamic element (timer/reaction)?
10. Threats scaled or stat-block anchors given; named inhabitants flagged for `rpg-npc-creator`?
11. ≥1 moral-cost hook at tone 3?
