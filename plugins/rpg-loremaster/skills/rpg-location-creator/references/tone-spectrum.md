# Tone Spectrum — the 1–5 Dark-Leaning Scale (location edition)

Defines the five tone levels this skill can generate. Default is **level 3 (dark-leaning)**. Read this whenever generating at a level other than 3, or whenever unsure how much shadow to put into a location. (Mirrors the kit's shared scale, reframed for location elements — premise, inhabitants, hazards, the choice.)

## The scale

| Level | Label | Reference works | What it feels like |
|---|---|---|---|
| **1** | Luminous classical | classic dungeon crawls, Dragonlance | A clear threat to beat, treasure to win, danger that's exciting not bleak. |
| **2** | Heroic adventure | Pathfinder default dungeons | Real danger and some grimness, but a clean victory is expected. |
| **3 ⭐ default** | **Dark-leaning** | Curse of Strahd's Death House, Strange Aeons sites, Witcher 3 caves | A place with a sad or sinister history; treasure that costs; an inhabitant you might pity. Light and shadow alternate. |
| **4** | Dark fantasy | Berserk's haunted ruins, darker modules | Mostly oppressive; victories are pyrrhic; the place wounds those who enter. |
| **5** | Grimdark | the worst horror modules | The location is a trap; "winning" is escaping with less than you came with. |

## How each level changes the location

### Level 1 (Luminous classical)
- Premise is straightforward (a monster moved in; bandits hole up here).
- Inhabitants are clearly hostile; clearing them is uncomplicated good.
- Hazards are obstacles, not horror.
- Treasure is a clean reward.
- "Why not cleared?" is simple (the recent / a guardian to beat).

### Level 2 (Heroic adventure)
- One complication (a captive, an inhabitant with a reason).
- A hazard or two with real teeth.
- The secret is mildly surprising.
- 1 hook carries ambiguity.

### Level 3 ⭐ (Dark-leaning — DEFAULT)
- **Mix 5:3:2** — the place is dangerous and rewarding, with ambiguity and a real shadow.
- **A sad or sinister history** — the premise carries weight (a massacre, a betrayal, a faith that turned). The "why happened" is tragic, not just "a dragon moved in."
- **At least one inhabitant is sympathetic** — defending young, enslaved, bound, a trapped survivor, the dead who deserve rest. Not everything here deserves the sword.
- **At least one haunt or "wrong" area** — a spot that's unsettling without combat.
- **Treasure costs something** — at least one prize is cursed, sacred, contested, or evidence; taking it is a choice.
- **The choice/dilemma is present** — the location forces a decision with no clean answer (free the bound thing or leave it; rob the grave to go deeper; the cure is also a weapon).
- A paladin of Iomedae should be able to clear this place *and* leave troubled by part of it.

### Level 4 (Dark fantasy)
- Mix flips to 2:3:5 — the place is mostly horror with a few mercies.
- The inhabitants are mostly victims-turned-monsters; the line between threat and tragedy blurs.
- Hazards and haunts dominate; the architecture itself is hostile.
- Treasure is mostly tainted; the clean prize is rare.
- The dilemma has no good option, only lesser harms.

### Level 5 (Grimdark)
- Mix 0:2:8 — no clean reward, no innocent inhabitant.
- The location is a predator; the premise is an atrocity.
- Survival, not victory, is the goal.
- Add a content advisory in the off-stage notes.

## Applying level 3 specifically (the default checklist)
1. **The premise has weight** — the "what happened" is tragic or sinister, not generic.
2. **One sympathetic inhabitant** — something here you might not want to kill.
3. **At least one haunt / "wrong" area** — dread without a fight.
4. **Treasure that costs** — one prize is cursed/sacred/contested/evidence.
5. **The choice** — a dilemma the place forces, no clean answer.
6. **≥1 moral-cost hook** — clearing it harms someone, or the thing inside is sympathetic.

The goal of level 3 is **contrast**: real treasure and real danger, with a history that makes the place *matter* and an inhabitant that makes the PCs hesitate. Pure light = a loot dungeon. Pure shadow = a meat grinder. Dark-leaning = a place that costs you something to clear, even when you win.

## Common mistakes
- **Over-darkening.** If the user asked for 3 and you wrote a hopeless meat-grinder where everything is a victim and nothing can be won cleanly, that's a 4–5. Pull back: restore a real reward and a beatable threat.
- **Under-darkening.** If it's just "rooms, monsters, loot" with no sad history, no sympathetic inhabitant, no haunt, and no costly choice, it's a 1–2. Add the weight and the dilemma.
- **Decorative darkness.** Don't smear gore for shock. Every shadow element ties to the premise — the haunt re-enacts the massacre; the cursed prize belonged to the betrayer; the sympathetic monster is the last of what lived here.

## Sliding between levels
If the user wants a level-3 location but "darker in the deepest level," generate at base 3 and dial the lower areas up by one. Note the deviation in off-stage so downstream skills stay consistent.
