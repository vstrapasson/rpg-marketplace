# PF2e Canon Quick Reference (for location creation)

PF2e (post-Remaster) elements curated **for locations** — what fills a bounded site with a believable ecology, danger, and treasure. Pick by what the premise and ecology call for, not by browsing.

## 1. Creature families by location type & environment

Use [AoN Creatures](https://2e.aonprd.com/Creatures.aspx) and [AoN Monster Families](https://2e.aonprd.com/MonsterFamilies.aspx). Pick a *family* that fits the premise, not a grab-bag.

| Location / environment | Fitting families |
|---|---|
| **Crypt / tomb / necropolis** | Undead (skeletons, zombies, ghouls, wights, wraiths, graveknights), Mummies, Haunts |
| **Built dungeon / fortress** | a holding faction (cultists, bandits, soldiers), Devils (bound), constructs, Oozes in the forgotten parts |
| **Temple (turned/sacred)** | Cultists + their patron's servitors (Fiends, aberrant things), Hags, an Outsider the faith bound |
| **Mage tower / lab** | Constructs (golems, animated objects), Homunculi, summoned Elementals, an escaped experiment (Aberration) |
| **Cave / underdark** | Aberrations (xulgaths, gugs, neothelids), Oozes, Drow/Duergar, Fungus things, blind predators |
| **Ruin (surface)** | Scavengers (gnolls, kobolds, goblins), Fey reclaiming it, Plant creatures, what destroyed it lingering |
| **Lair (natural den)** | one apex (Dragon, Owlbear, Troll, Chimera) + its prey/scavengers; Drakes; pack hunters |
| **Manor / estate** | a Haunting, a Vampire/Dhampir line, Doppelgangers, wererats in the cellars, a family curse made flesh |
| **Flooded / coastal site** | Sahuagin, Merfolk, aquatic Undead, Krakens (deep), Ceratioidi, drowned dead |
| **Swamp / sunken** | Boggards, Lizardfolk, Hags (Sea/Green), Will-o'-Wisps, Hydras |

**Dark-leaning texture (include at least one at tone 3):** a Haunt; an Undead or Fiend or Aberration; a Hag; a creature that is *sympathetic* (defending young, enslaved, bound against its will).

## 2. Building an ecology (predator / prey / rival)

A living location has *relationships*, not a monster per room:
- **Dominant** — who holds the place (the faction/creatures from a handoff, or the apex predator).
- **Prey / resource** — what the dominant group eats or exploits (vermin, captives, fungus, a bound elemental powering the place).
- **Rival / wedge** — something *else* living here that conflicts with the dominant group (a rival pack in the depths, a haunt the cult fears, an imprisoned thing). This is the PCs' lever — they can ally, provoke, or play both sides.
- **The scavengers** — the small things that move in the margins (rats, sprites, oozes, carrion-eaters) — texture and early-warning.

Ask: what do they eat? where's their water? who's in charge? what are they afraid of? The answers fill rooms with purpose.

## 3. Hazards & haunts

The other half of danger — lethal *without* combat, and tone-rich. See [AoN Hazards](https://2e.aonprd.com/Hazards.aspx).
- **Environmental:** cave-in, flooding, bad air/suffocation, lava, unstable floors, cold.
- **Magical:** cursed altar, summoning rune, spectral guardian, a ward gone sour, a trapped soul.
- **Haunts:** a ghost re-enacting its death at a specific spot — the bridge where the drowned call, the cell that whispers, the nursery that rocks itself. Perfect for tone 3, no fight required, and a clue to the premise.

At tone 3+, at least one haunt or a "wrong" area (the room where sound dies; the stair that's one step too long).

## 4. Traps

Classic site danger — best when they reflect *who built them and why*. A tomb's traps protect the dead; a thieves' vault's traps are practical and deadly; a mad wizard's traps are capricious. See [AoN Hazards](https://2e.aonprd.com/Hazards.aspx) (traps live there). Don't over-trap — a few meaningful, telegraphed traps beat a corridor of save-or-die.

## 5. Planar leakage (the supernatural source)

When a location holds something supernatural, it traces to a plane — and that source is often the secret. See [AoN Planes](https://2e.aonprd.com/Planes.aspx).
- **Ethereal / Boneyard** → hauntings, the unquiet dead, a thin "veil."
- **Shadow Plane** → a place "gone wrong," gothic, light that doesn't work right.
- **Hell / Abyss / Abaddon** → a bound fiend, a summoning chamber, a rift.
- **First World** → fey reclaiming a ruin, a place that's too old, time running strange.
- **Negative/Void energy** → why the undead persist; the well that drains the living.

**Pattern:** "why is this supernatural thing *here*?" → a rift, a binding, a relic, a death. The answer is usually the location's deepest secret and a PC lever.

## 6. Treasure tied to premise

Treasure means more when it fits the place:
- **Tomb** → grave-goods, a signet, a sword buried with its lord, *the cost of robbing the dead*.
- **Temple** → relics, offerings, a holy/unholy item, *consecrated and watched*.
- **Mage tower** → scrolls, a wand, a dangerous half-finished item, *a captured experiment*.
- **Vault / guild** → coin, ledgers, blackmail, a deed — *information as treasure*.
- **Lair** → swallowed loot from past victims, a hoard, *bones to identify the missing*.

Make at least one prize **cost or mean something** (cursed, contested, sacred, evidence) — that's the tone-3 choice.

## 7. NPC stat-block anchors & scaling

Sketch named inhabitants' *personalities*; for mechanics, point at reusable PF2e blocks ([AoN Creatures](https://2e.aonprd.com/Creatures.aspx) / [AoN Monster Families](https://2e.aonprd.com/MonsterFamilies.aspx)) and **scale to the party level** if known:

| Role in the location | Reusable PF2e block to suggest |
|---|---|
| Boss / dominant leader | the apex creature, or a Cult Leader / Crime Lord / named monster bumped to level |
| Lieutenant / mini-boss | a mid-tier creature of the family, or a Knight/Mage block |
| Rank-and-file inhabitants | Guard, Cultist, Bandit, Skeleton, Zombie, Goblin, etc. |
| The bound/sympathetic thing | a creature reskinned; flag for `rpg-npc-creator` |
| Trapped survivor / captive | Commoner, Acolyte, or a relevant NPC block |

Tell the user: "Use the **[creature]** block at level X, or bump it to match your party." Don't write attack bonuses or HP yourself. For encounter balance, suggest the GM check the PF2e encounter-building guidelines (XP budget by threat) rather than guessing.

## Anti-pattern reminder
A location is **premise + ecology**, not a monster zoo or a loot list. Reach into this file to build *relationships* (who eats whom, who fears what) and to anchor the *one supernatural source* — not to scatter unrelated creatures and treasure across rooms.
