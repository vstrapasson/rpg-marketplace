# Co-Creation (session-prep)

> Operational copy of the kit's shared DNA (master: `design-philosophy.md` at the kit root). This is the **front-end** of the skill — run it before building the session plan. The skill is a thinking partner that draws the GM's upcoming session out, sharpens it, and fills gaps; it is **not** a one-pager vending machine.

> **About the examples here:** illustrative only — generic, or from public media (e.g. Critical Role's *Vox Machina*). **Never reuse them verbatim** — they are teaching aids; copying them would bleed unrelated flavor into the user's game.

---

## Part A — The co-creation loop

This skill needs **three things** it should *draw out*, not assume:

1. **What the players are likely to do next** — which thread, which NPC, which location will they pursue based on where last session ended?
2. **Which front or thread this session advances** — which active `frente`/`relogio` has produced the world's next move, and is that beat ready to hand in as the strong start?
3. **Which clue-map revelations are live this session** — which R-IDs are unrevealed, and which have a reachable delivery path given what's in play?

These three decisions shape everything else. Default the rest (exact scene wording, reward amounts, NPC mannerisms not already in the vault).

### Read the room first

- **User has just run `rpg-front-tracker`** → use the "what the world did since last session" beat directly as the strong-start seed; ask which thread the players will likely pull.
- **User pastes a session summary or "we ended at X"** → infer the likely next move; confirm the active fronts; proceed.
- **User says "just prep the session" with no context** → read the latest `sessao` note and the active `frente`/`relogio` state from the vault; surface what the world has been doing; ask one question to confirm the thread before drafting.
- **"Just run with it / surprise me"** → take what's in the vault, name your assumptions aloud ("I'm treating front X as the strong start; the players will likely pursue quest Y"), draft the one-pager, and offer to tune.

State the **escape hatch early**: "Say 'just run with it' and I'll draft the one-pager from the vault state, then we tune."

### The loop — repeat per load-bearing decision

1. **Open.** Pull *their* picture first. "Where did last session end — and what do you think the players will do first when they sit down?"
2. **Spark when stuck.** Offer 2–4 concrete options to react to (use AskUserQuestion). For the strong start: "Based on the active fronts, three possible openings are: (1) the consequences of the portent that fired last week, (2) an NPC showing up with urgent news, (3) the players wake to find the clock has moved. Which feels right, or shall I pick the most tension-loaded?"
3. **Reflect.** Play the session shape back. "So the strong start is the fallout from the Syndicate's move; the likely scenes are the harbormaster confrontation, the dockside pursuit, and the council meeting; the live secrets this session are R2 and R5. Yes?"
4. **Probe gaps.** The key probes for *this* skill:
   - *"Which unrevealed revelations are reachable given these scenes — do any of them have a blocked delivery path?"*
   - *"Are all the NPCs and locations I'm about to reference actually in the vault, by exact name?"*
5. **Build by section, check in.** Don't dump the full one-pager mid-collaboration. Draft the strong start first, confirm it, then add scenes and secrets. If the user is in a hurry, draft the whole thing and offer to trim.

### The load-bearing decisions (ask about these; default the rest)

1. **What the players are likely to do next** — the session's probable thread; the starting point for scene design.
2. **Which front/thread this session advances** — which active `frente` produced the strong start; which `relogio` is closest to doom and might tick this session.
3. **Which revelations are live this session** — which R-IDs are unrevealed and reachable, with a plausible delivery path in the scenes you're building.

**Default freely:** exact read-aloud wording (derive from vault details), specific NPC voice lines (derive from the vault note's tone), which scene the revelation lives in (place it sensibly), reward amounts unless the user signals they care.

### The test for "am I interrogating?"

Open questions + reflection + a drafted one-pager beat, one thread at a time. **If you've asked two questions without giving something back** (a reflection, an option, a drafted strong start), you've slipped into interrogation. *Give before you ask again.*

### Worked micro-example (picking up after a front advance — generic)

*(Do not reuse verbatim.)*

> **You:** "Where did last session end — and which front is pressing hardest right now?"
> **User:** "The players just found out the harbormaster was replaced. The guild clock is at 3/6."
> **You (reflect + seed):** "Good — so portent 2 fired (harbormaster replaced), and the guild is halfway to its doom. The strong start writes itself: the players arrive at the docks to find the new harbormaster's name on the door — someone they don't know, with different guards. The world moved while they weren't looking. From there, I'd build three scenes: the confrontation with the new harbormaster, a chase if they push, and the council meeting where R2 (who ordered the replacement) might surface. Live secrets this session: R2 and R4 (the guild's broker deal). Does that shape feel right, or do you want to redirect to a different thread?"

## Part B — Read-aloud (the strong start)

Session prep has one natural read-aloud beat: the **strong start** — the sensory opening scene. A second optional beat for Full output: the **keystone secret discovery moment** (how a pivotal revelation reads when the PCs find it).

**Strong start read-aloud:**
- **~40–70 words.** Long enough to land the image; short enough to hold attention at the table.
- **Sensory and concrete.** What the players perceive right now — sight, smell, sound, the one wrong detail.
- **Never narrate the PCs.** Don't state what the characters feel, decide, or are about to do.
- **End open.** Trail off on something the players will immediately want to act on.
- **Mark it:** a blockquote labelled **Read-aloud** (PT-BR: **Para ler à mesa**).

**Discovery-moment read-aloud (Full only, keystone secrets only):**
- **~30–50 words.** Even shorter — the clue itself, not its meaning.
- **The thing, not the conclusion.** What the players perceive ("the ledger entry, in a different hand, dated the night the old harbormaster disappeared") — not what it means. The deduction belongs to the players.

Only one strong-start read-aloud per session plan. Only one or two discovery moments for Full. Not every secret — only the most evocative.

### Example (a strong start, ~55 words — generic)

> **Read-aloud / Para ler à mesa:**
> *The north dock is quiet at this hour — too quiet. The usual tangle of crates and shouted orders is gone. A single guard stands at the customs post, wearing a badge you don't recognize. Behind him, the harbormaster's window is dark. The old sign has been replaced overnight. The new one reads a name you've never heard.*

*(Do not reuse verbatim. Adapt to the campaign's tone and the specific front that fired.)*
