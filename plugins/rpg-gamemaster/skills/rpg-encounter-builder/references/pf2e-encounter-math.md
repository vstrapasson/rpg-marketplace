# PF2e Encounter Math — Verified Reference

> Numbers verified against Archives of Nethys on build; PF2e is errata-sensitive — re-verify if a discrepancy appears at the table.

> **Source:** Pathfinder 2e GM Core — Building Encounters / Treasure. See Archives of Nethys (https://2e.aonprd.com/Rules.aspx) for the authoritative current text.

> **Examples are illustrative — never reuse verbatim.** All worked examples use generic parties and generic creature descriptions. No private campaign content appears anywhere in this file.

---

## 1. XP Budget by Threat (Party of 4)

**Table 1: Encounter Budget**

| Threat    | XP Budget    | Character Adjustment |
|-----------|-------------|----------------------|
| Trivial   | 40 or less  | 10 or less           |
| Low       | 60          | 20                   |
| Moderate  | 80          | 20                   |
| Severe    | 120         | 30                   |
| Extreme   | 160         | 40                   |

**Party size adjustment:** The budget above assumes exactly 4 characters. For each PC beyond the fourth, add the Character Adjustment for that threat to the budget. For each PC below four, subtract the same amount. The adjustment is applied per missing or extra PC.

**Example adjustments:**
- 5-player party, Moderate threat: 80 + 20 = 100 XP budget
- 3-player party, Severe threat: 120 − 30 = 90 XP budget
- 6-player party, Extreme threat: 160 + 40 + 40 = 240 XP budget

## 2. Per-Creature XP by Level Relative to Party

**Table 2: Creature XP and Role (Table 10-2, GM Core)**

| Creature Level      | XP  | Suggested Role                              |
|--------------------|-----|---------------------------------------------|
| Party Level −4     | 10  | Low-threat lackey                           |
| Party Level −3     | 15  | Low- or moderate-threat lackey              |
| Party Level −2     | 20  | Any lackey or standard creature             |
| Party Level −1     | 30  | Any standard creature                       |
| Party Level        | 40  | Any standard creature or low-threat boss    |
| Party Level +1     | 60  | Low- or moderate-threat boss                |
| Party Level +2     | 80  | Moderate- or severe-threat boss             |
| Party Level +3     | 120 | Severe- or extreme-threat boss              |
| Party Level +4     | 160 | Extreme-threat solo boss                    |

**Range:** Creatures outside PL−4 to PL+4 can still appear, but those below PL−4 award no XP and are trivial. Creatures above PL+4 are beyond the normal design envelope.

## 3. Hazard XP

**Table 3: Hazard XP (Table 10-14, GM Core)**

| Hazard Level Relative to Party | Simple Hazard XP | Complex Hazard XP |
|-------------------------------|-----------------|-------------------|
| Party Level −4                | 2               | 10                |
| Party Level −3                | 3               | 15                |
| Party Level −2                | 4               | 20                |
| Party Level −1                | 6               | 30                |
| Party Level                   | 8               | 40                |
| Party Level +1                | 12              | 60                |
| Party Level +2                | 16              | 80                |
| Party Level +3                | 24              | 120               |
| Party Level +4                | 30              | 150               |

**Simple hazards** are one-fifth the XP of a complex hazard of the same level.

**Complex hazards** are treated as full encounter participants — the rule text states their XP equals "the XP for a monster of the same level." XP is awarded whether the party disables the hazard, avoids it, or endures its attacks. Triggering the same hazard again yields no additional XP.

**No-XP threshold:** Hazards below Party Level −4 are trivial and award no XP.

**Known discrepancy (flag for GM):** The GM Core rule text states complex hazard XP equals a monster of the same level, which would be 160 XP at PL+4. However, Table 10-14 in the source prints 150 XP for PL+4 complex hazards. The table value (150 XP) is what is printed and should be used; the rule text and table are inconsistent at this row. Re-verify on Archives of Nethys — Hazards (https://2e.aonprd.com/Hazards.aspx) if this matters for your encounter.

**Treasure includes:** coins, gems, art objects, and the market value of magic items found (not purchased). The GM Core guidance is to award the total-per-level as a budget across all encounters and rewards in a level, not strictly per-encounter.

## 5. The Honesty Rules

When using this reference to build an encounter or advise a GM:

1. **Always show the budget arithmetic.** State the threat level chosen, the starting XP budget, any party-size adjustments applied, and how each creature or hazard contributes to the total.
2. **Always state the assumed party level and size.** Every encounter recommendation is meaningless without them.
3. **The math must be exact.** If a number is uncertain or a discrepancy is suspected, say so explicitly and point the GM to verify on Archives of Nethys — Rules (https://2e.aonprd.com/Rules.aspx).
4. **Never round silently.** If a calculation produces a non-round number (e.g., from a party-size adjustment), state it and let the GM decide how to handle it.
5. **Flag errata sensitivity.** PF2e has been remastered; some older community sources still show pre-Remaster values. Prefer the current AoN tables.

## 6. Worked Example — Full Arithmetic

**Scenario:** A level-3 party of 4 characters, Moderate-threat encounter.

**Step 1 — Set the budget.**

Party level: 3. Party size: 4 (no adjustment needed). Threat: Moderate.

From Table 1: Moderate budget for 4 = **80 XP**.

**Step 2 — Select creatures.**

The GM wants a classic "boss + soldiers" structure. Available creature options at level 3:

| Creature Description              | Creature Level | Relative Level | XP (from Table 2) |
|-----------------------------------|---------------|---------------|-------------------|
| Veteran mercenary captain (boss)  | 4             | PL+1          | 60                |
| Mercenary soldier (standard)      | 2             | PL−1          | 30                |

The GM picks one boss and one soldier:

| Creature          | Count | XP Each | Subtotal |
|-------------------|-------|---------|----------|
| Captain (PL+1)    | 1     | 60      | 60       |
| Soldier (PL−1)    | 1     | 30      | 30       |
| **Total**         |       |         | **90**   |

**Step 3 — Check against budget.**

90 XP > 80 XP. The encounter is 10 XP over budget — this tips it toward the high end of Moderate, close to Severe (120). The GM has two options:

- Drop the soldier → 60 XP (Low threat). Too easy.
- Replace the captain (PL+1, 60 XP) with a standard creature (PL, 40 XP) and keep the soldier (PL−1, 30 XP) → 40 + 30 = **70 XP**. Within Moderate (60–80 range), slightly easy side.
- Keep captain, replace soldier with a weaker lackey (PL−2, 20 XP) → 60 + 20 = **80 XP exactly**. On budget.

**Final build (on budget):**

| Creature          | Count | XP Each | Subtotal |
|-------------------|-------|---------|----------|
| Captain (PL+1)    | 1     | 60      | 60       |
| Lackey (PL−2)     | 1     | 20      | 20       |
| **Total**         |       |         | **80 XP** |

80 XP = Moderate budget for party of 4 at level 3. Arithmetic verified.

**Treasure allotment for this encounter (optional):** At level 3, Moderate encounter = **50 gp** (from Table 4).
