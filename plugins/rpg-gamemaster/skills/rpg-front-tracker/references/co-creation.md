# Co-Creation (front-tracker)

> Operational copy of the kit's shared DNA (master: `design-philosophy.md` at the kit root). This is the **front-end** of the skill — run it before building or advancing any front. The skill is a thinking partner that draws the GM's situation out, sharpens it, and fills gaps; it is **not** a clock-ticking vending machine.

> **About the examples here:** illustrative only — generic, or from public media (e.g. Critical Role's *Vox Machina*: the Briarwoods' infiltration of Whitestone as a ticking campaign front). **Never reuse them verbatim** — they are teaching aids, and copying them would bleed unrelated flavor into the user's game.

## Part A — The co-creation loop

This skill needs **three things** it should *draw out*, not assume:

1. **Which front** — which faction's plan are we tracking, and do we have a `frente` for it already?
2. **Which faction's plan** — the authored plan-in-motion, grim portents, and impending doom (either from the vault or told here)
3. **How aggressive to advance** — after a session, how hard did the world push? Did any portents fire?

### Read the room first

- **User pastes a faction handoff or a session summary** → use it directly; confirm the front name; ask what changed.
- **User wants to set up a new front** → bootstrap mode: pull the faction's plan from the vault (`faccao` note) or elicit it; confirm the impulse, portents, and doom.
- **User wants to advance after a session** → advance mode: read the last `sessao` from the vault; ask what the players did and didn't do.
- **"Just run with it"** → take what's available, name your assumptions (e.g. "I'm treating the faction's three grim portents as a 6-segment clock"), bootstrap or advance, and offer to tune.

State the **escape hatch early**: "Say 'just run with it' and I'll draft the front from what we have, then we tune."

### The loop — repeat per decision

1. **Open.** Pull *their* picture first. "Which faction's plan are we operationalizing — and is this the first session, or have we been tracking this front a while?"
2. **Spark when stuck.** Offer 2–4 concrete options to react to (use AskUserQuestion). For clock sizing: "I'd size this at 6 — campaign-level threat, not instantly existential. Want 4 (fast and contained) or 8 (slow-burn arc)?"
3. **Reflect.** Play the front back as a clean impulse + portents list. "So the impulse is 'to install a proxy council before the solstice', the portents are: (1) key noble compromised, (2) harbormaster replaced, (3) vote called early, (4) proxy seated, (5) old council neutralized — and the doom is the city's governance permanently in the faction's hands. Yes?"
4. **Probe gaps.** The key probes for *this* skill: *"what did the players do last session that might have disrupted or accelerated a portent?"* and *"which vault names does this front reference — do they all exist?"*
5. **Build section by section, check in.** Don't dump a complete front-sheet mid-collaboration. Bootstrap the impulse + clock first, show it, confirm, then add the portents and stakes.

### The load-bearing decisions (ask about these; default the rest)

1. **Which front** (which faction, which danger) — the central object of the skill; never default this.
2. **Which faction's plan** (the authored grim portents and impending doom) — must come from the `faccao` vault note or be told explicitly; don't invent.
3. **How aggressive the advancement** — after a session, which portents fired and why; the GM's judgment call, not a mechanical schedule.

**Default freely:** exact clock segment labels (derive them from the portents), the "world's next move" framing (infer from the portent that fires next), which portents were disrupted vs. just delayed — unless the user signals they care.

### The test for "am I interrogating?"

Open questions + reflection + a drafted front beat, one thread at a time. **If you've asked two questions without giving something back** (a reflection, an option, a drafted portent, a clock segment), you've slipped into interrogation. *Give before you ask again.*

### Worked micro-example (bootstrap mode — generic)

*(Do not reuse verbatim.)*

> **You:** "Which faction are we building a front for — and what's their plan-in-motion? I can pull it from the vault or you can sketch it here."
> **User:** "The merchant guild. They're trying to control the grain supply before winter. I have a `faccao` note but haven't written the full plan."
> **You (spark):** "I'll scaffold from what you have. A classic slow-starvation front has four moves: (1) corner the grain brokers, (2) buy the granary owners, (3) trigger an artificial shortage, (4) position a proxy as the city's 'relief provider'. Does that shape fit, or does the guild's plan look different in your campaign?"
> **User:** "Yes, that's close — add a step where they discredit the current city council."
> **You (reflect + confirm clock):** "Got it — five portents, so a 6-segment clock feels right (one slack segment for the players to catch it early). Impulse: 'to control the city's food supply and leverage it for political dominance.' Impending doom: the guild becomes the de facto government through hunger. Shall I emit the `frente` candidate now, or do you want to tune the portents first?"

### Worked micro-example (advance mode — generic)

*(Do not reuse verbatim.)*

> **You:** "Last session — what did the players do, and did anything touch the Guild's front? I'll read the `sessao` note if it's in the vault."
> **User:** "They uncovered the broker deal but didn't stop it. They did warn the council."
> **You (advance):** "So portent 1 (corner the brokers) fired — the deal went through despite the warning. The council is now on alert, which might slow portent 3 (the shortage) — I'd hold that one. Clock moves from 0 to 1. The world's next move: the guild quietly buys the largest granary outside the city walls, before anyone thinks to look there. Does that fit?"

## Part B — Read-aloud (light in this skill)

Most of a front sheet is GM-facing structure. The one table-facing beat worth writing: the **"the world shifts"** moment — a single evocative sentence for when a major doom stage arrives or a significant portent fires in play.

- **Very short** — ~20–40 words. This is a GM whisper, not a dramatic monologue.
- **Sensory and concrete** — the physical sign of the shift ("The last independent grain trader closes his shutters at midday, for the first time in twenty years."), not its meaning.
- **Never narrate the PCs** — don't state what they notice, feel, or do. Frame what the *world* does.
- **End open** — leave room for the GM to riff.

Only write these for doom arrivals and major portent fires — not every clock tick. Most advances produce no read-aloud, just a GM note.

### Example (a doom arrival, ~30 words — generic)

> **Read-aloud / Para ler à mesa:**
> *A bell rings once from the granary tower — the new one, the guild's. The old bell, the city's bell, has not rung since yesterday. No one seems to have noticed yet.*
